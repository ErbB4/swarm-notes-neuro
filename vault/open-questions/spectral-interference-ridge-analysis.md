title: Spectral Interference in Ridge Analysis
---

**Background:** The adaptive harmonic model assumes signals are composed of oscillatory components with time-varying amplitudes and phases, which are typically analyzed by tracking local maxima (ridges) in a scalogram. Defining and extracting these ridges reliably becomes mathematically ambiguous when the instantaneous frequencies of different components are close or crossover.

**Question / Future Work:** A significant challenge in nonstationary signal analysis is the failure of ridge extraction when instantaneous frequencies (IFs) are not well-separated. Further research is required to extend probabilistic ridge analysis frameworks to handle spectral overlap, intersection, and non-slowly varying components.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental limitation in time-frequency analysis for complex signals, affecting the reliability of component separation and tracking.

**Evidence:** Extending the theory to settings with spectral interference; that is, when IFs are close or intersect, remains an open problem for future ridge analysis.
