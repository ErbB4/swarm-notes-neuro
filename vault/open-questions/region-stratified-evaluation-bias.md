title: Region-Stratified Performance Evaluation
---

**Background:** Monitoring station density is inherently biased toward well-instrumented regions, while under-instrumented regions suffer from higher observation missingness. There is no established methodology for effectively evaluating models specifically for their generalization across these disparate regional sensing environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to develop region-stratified evaluation methods where models are specifically tested on their performance across different levels of station density and missingness, moving beyond global aggregate metrics that may mask poor performance in under-instrumented areas.

**Why It Matters:** Vital to ensure that air-quality forecasting models are equitable and effective globally, rather than just being optimized for wealthy, well-monitored urban centers.

**Evidence:** To make this bias explicit, we report continent-level station distribution and missingness statistics in Appendix F and propose region-stratified evaluation as a future benchmark track.
