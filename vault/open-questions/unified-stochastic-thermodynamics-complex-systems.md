title: Unified Framework for Complex Systems
---

**Background:** Stochastic thermodynamics aims to describe non-equilibrium processes using thermodynamic quantities like entropy production, but its standard foundations, rooted in energy dissipation and the local detailed balance principle, face significant challenges when applied to macroscopic or non-physical complex systems where such energy links are not direct.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open question whether a unified formulation of stochastic thermodynamics exists that can provide robust and quantitatively meaningful insights for macroscopic, biological, or non-physical systems where the connection to energy dissipation is not direct. Specifically, it is unclear to what extent statistical irreversibility, used as a proxy for dissipation in these complex domains, can be reconciled with the field's traditional thermodynamic foundations.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the fundamental scope and validity of the field as it attempts to move from a theory of microscopic physical fluctuations to a general framework for complex system dynamics across disciplines.
