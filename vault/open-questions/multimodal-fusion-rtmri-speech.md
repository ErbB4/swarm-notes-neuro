title: Effective Multimodal Fusion Architectures
---

**Background:** Multimodal learning often involves integrating acoustic and articulatory data, yet simple concatenation methods frequently fail to achieve improvements over unimodal acoustic models in speech-related tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining effective architectures and training strategies for fusing acoustic and visual articulatory information remains an open challenge, specifically regarding how to overcome performance degradation when multimodal models underperform compared to unimodal acoustic baselines.

**Why It Matters:** Current multimodal approaches on rtMRI datasets suffer from performance regressions, indicating a lack of optimal fusion strategies that can leverage articulatory data to complement rather than distract from acoustic models.

**Evidence:** We encourage future work to explore alternative approaches to multimodal learning, such as cross-modal attention and multimodal mixture-of-experts.
