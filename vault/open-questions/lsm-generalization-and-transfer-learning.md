title: Generalization of Large Spectrum Models
---

**Background:** While decoder-only transformer architectures demonstrate effectiveness in spectrum forecasting, the reliance on high-fidelity, large-scale RF datasets remains a significant deployment barrier in diverse geographical environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to evaluate the zero-shot or few-shot generalization capabilities of large spectrum models (LSMs) in entirely new, unseen environments without the need for additional fine-tuning data. Investigating whether these models can extract universal features of wireless signal patterns across diverse radio environments remains a critical, unresolved research question.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the cross-site generalization capability of LSMs is essential to determine if these models can serve as foundational, ready-to-deploy intelligence for dynamic spectrum access without requiring extensive site-specific training.
