title: Extending to Continuous Spatial Domains
---

**Background:** Point process convergence results have been established for univariate and general multivariate time series, yet extensions to spatially indexed time series remain underdeveloped. The modeling of extreme events in space and time requires limit theorems for spatio-temporal point processes that account for both temporal and spatial dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending current convergence results for spatio-temporal point processes of exceedances to continuous spatial domains requires theoretical development to replace current assumptions restricted to discrete and finite sets of sites.

**Why It Matters:** Most environmental and climate modeling applications involve continuous spatial domains, making the extension of discrete-site limit theorems critical for broad applicability.

**Evidence:** In this case, not only the time t, but also the site s of the occurrence of an extreme event is of interest. However, limit results for such spatio-temporal point processes are still missing in the literature.
