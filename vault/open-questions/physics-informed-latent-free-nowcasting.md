title: Physics-Informed Latent-Free Precipitation Nowcasting
---

**Background:** Precipitation nowcasting frameworks often face a trade-off between inference efficiency and high-fidelity detail preservation, particularly when using generative models that rely on latent space compression to perform dimensionality reduction.

**Question / Future Work:** While existing latent-free generative models utilizing Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks show promise in capturing complex meteorological dynamics, there is a need to integrate explicit atmospheric physical laws as prior knowledge into these models. Investigating how to synergize the nonlinear approximation capacity of learnable splines with physics-based regularization could lead to more robust and physically consistent forecasting frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Integrating physical consistency into purely data-driven generative models is essential for improving the reliability of extreme weather nowcasting and ensuring that predictions adhere to the underlying governing dynamics of atmospheric systems.
