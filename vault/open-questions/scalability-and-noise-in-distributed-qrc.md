title: Scalability of Distributed QRC
---

**Background:** Distributed quantum computing architectures allow for the parallel execution of sub-components across multiple quantum processing units (QPUs). The impact of various distribution configurations on noise accumulation and overall model performance remains a subject of ongoing investigation in quantum reservoir computing.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to systematically investigate the performance of distributed quantum reservoir computing models when scaled to much larger dimensions and across diverse quantum hardware, particularly the relationship between component distribution, noise mitigation, and scaling limits.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the scaling limits and noise behavior of distributed quantum systems is critical for practical NISQ-era application development. Determining how hardware-agnostic partitioning influences noise accumulation informs the design of robust, modular quantum machine learning pipelines.

**Evidence:** Moreover, extending these distributed RC models to larger-scale ones and testing them on different quantum hardware constitute important aspects for further study.
