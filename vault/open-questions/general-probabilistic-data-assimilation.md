title: General Probabilistic Data Assimilation
---

**Background:** Rainfall estimation remains a complex challenge for deep learning due to the localized, intermittent, and skewed nature of precipitation, as well as the inherent noise and irregularity of heterogeneous data sources like weather stations and remote sensing. While current Neural Process-based models show promise for spatio-temporal densification, they often struggle with real-world complexities such as varying sensor quality, missing data, and the need for seamless multi-modal integration.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to evolve current probabilistic data assimilation frameworks beyond integrating meteorological variables as simple model inputs. Future research should investigate incorporating these variables as predictive targets to enable a more holistic, general-purpose probabilistic assimilation system capable of handling complex interactions between diverse atmospheric variables.

**Why It Matters:** This is a significant step toward moving beyond task-specific rainfall densification models to more robust, unified systems capable of handling multi-variable, long-term atmospheric modeling and data assimilation in a principled, uncertainty-aware manner.
