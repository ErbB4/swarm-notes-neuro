title: Quantile Calibration Dispersion Trade-off
---

**Background:** In end-to-end forecasting models that jointly train deterministic and probabilistic heads, there is an inherent tension between the objectives.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to determine how joint optimization of probabilistic and deterministic heads impacts the diversity and dispersion of stochastic ensemble members. Specifically, investigating whether the probabilistic branch overly constrains the stochastic sampling space, thereby limiting the performance gains typically realized through ensemble aggregation, is critical for future model design.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this trade-off is essential for developing models that simultaneously deliver high-accuracy point forecasts and robust, well-calibrated uncertainty estimates without compromising the benefits of ensemble averaging.
