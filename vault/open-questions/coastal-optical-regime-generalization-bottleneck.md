title: Coastal Optical Regime Generalization
---

**Background:** Coastal water quality monitoring via remote sensing is limited by the variability of regional bio-optical relationships, which constrain the transferability of retrieval algorithms across diverse water bodies. Current approaches often require significant in situ training data, which are resource-intensive to collect.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should focus on diversifying the set of study sites to represent a wider range of global optical water types, as current evaluations are often restricted to narrow geographic subsets. This expansion is essential for establishing the robustness and true global generalizability of physics-informed retrieval frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Generalization across diverse global water types is a fundamental challenge for the scalability of hyperspectral remote sensing.
