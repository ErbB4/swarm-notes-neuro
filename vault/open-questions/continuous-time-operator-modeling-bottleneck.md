title: Continuous-Time Operator Modeling Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Modeling continuous-time stochastic processes using discrete-time Markov chains imposes rigid grid discretization, which fails to capture high-frequency market microstructural dynamics. Integrating continuous-time formalisms into structured neural frameworks remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should investigate extending the operator-first neural framework into continuous time, potentially using neural stochastic differential equations. This would accommodate irregular observation intervals and provide a more natural representation of financial microstructure.

**Why It Matters:** Technically necessary to align structured operator modeling with the nature of continuous-time stochastic calculus and high-frequency trading data.

**Evidence:** A longer-term direction is to extend the transparent operator view into continuous time (e.g., via neural stochastic differential equations), allowing for irregular observation intervals and a more natural treatment of market microstructure.
