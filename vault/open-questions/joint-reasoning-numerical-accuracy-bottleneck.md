title: Joint Reasoning-Numerical Accuracy Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Time series forecasting involves a inherent trade-off between the flexible, general-purpose reasoning capabilities of large language models and the precise, domain-specific numerical accuracy required for forecasting tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** Maintaining high-level semantic reasoning for agentic planning while simultaneously achieving domain-specific numerical precision remains an unresolved challenge in agentic time series modeling. Achieving a robust, cross-domain balance between these distinct cognitive requirements without performance degradation in either domain is not fully realized.

**Why It Matters:** This captures the fundamental bottleneck in current agentic LLM-forecasting frameworks, specifically regarding the interaction between frozen reasoning models and fine-tuned numerical predictors.
