title: Generalizing PDE Learning Methods
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal prediction models often rely on recurrent architectures or physics-informed constraints to approximate underlying partial differential equations (PDEs). Learning these governing dynamics remains difficult when the underlying systems are complex or the governing equations are unknown.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a persistent challenge in developing neural architectures that can sufficiently approximate governing PDEs of dynamical systems without falling into the trap of over-specialization to specific domain priors, necessitating a focus on improving the representative capacity and adaptability of models for complex, unobserved physical dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this bottleneck is essential for the move from task-specific PINNs to general-purpose foundation models for physical dynamics.
