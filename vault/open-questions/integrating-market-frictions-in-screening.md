title: Integrating Market Frictions in Screening
---

**Background:** Two-stage portfolio procedures require a first-stage screening step to reduce dimensionality followed by a second-stage optimization; these steps are often evaluated without accounting for the cumulative impact of trading costs, portfolio turnover, and market liquidity constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating market frictions such as portfolio turnover, transaction costs, and liquidity constraints into the screening criteria of two-stage portfolio procedures remains an open challenge. Future work must bridge the gap between theoretical asset selection performance and the practical feasibility of portfolios under real-world economic constraints.

**Why It Matters:** Without accounting for transaction costs and turnover, the reported portfolio gains may be overstated, and the selection method might be practically infeasible for high-frequency or high-turnover rebalancing strategies.
