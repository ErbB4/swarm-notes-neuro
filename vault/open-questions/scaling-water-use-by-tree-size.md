title: Scaling water-use by size
---

**Background:** Tree water-use does not necessarily scale linearly with tree size, and the relationship between tree dimensions and physiological water transport is complex and species-dependent.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining how to effectively incorporate tree size, crown structure, and competition dynamics to scale water-use predictions from individual trees to broader forest stands remains an unresolved issue. Improving this scaling requires capturing how morphological characteristics modulate the relationship between sap flux density and environmental drivers.

**Why It Matters:** Scaling individual-level physiological models to stand-level estimates is a critical bottleneck for operationalizing precision irrigation and resource management.
