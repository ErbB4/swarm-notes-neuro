title: Probabilistic Building Load Forecasting
---

**Background:** Building load forecasting relies on complex, non-linear, and often volatile data influenced by human behavior and environmental factors. Current deterministic forecasting models face challenges in fully capturing the uncertainty inherent in these time-series predictions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to evolve existing deterministic forecasting architectures into probabilistic forecasting models to better quantify predictive uncertainty in building energy demand. This transition is essential for providing more reliable decision-support information for grid operators and aggregators.

**Why It Matters:** Probabilistic forecasting is crucial for robust risk management and grid balancing in the presence of high load volatility, which deterministic models struggle to capture adequately.

**Evidence:** Subsequent investigations will also explore expanding this deterministic architecture into a probabilistic forecasting model to quantify predictive uncertainty...
