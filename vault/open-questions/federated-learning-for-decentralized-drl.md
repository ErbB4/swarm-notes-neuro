title: Federated Learning for Decentralized DRL
---

**Background:** Federated learning enables multiple edge nodes to collaboratively train a shared model without exchanging raw local data.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to investigate federated or collaborative learning mechanisms that allow distributed agents in a computing continuum to share knowledge or improve model convergence while maintaining scalability and ensuring data privacy among participants.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical bottleneck for scaling multi-agent DRL in large-scale distributed edge environments where privacy concerns or communication overhead prevent centralized model training or raw data exchange.

**Evidence:** First, future studies may directly investigate federated or collaborative learning mechanisms to allow distributed agents to share knowledge while preserving system scalability and privacy.
