title: Longitudinal Clinical Monitoring via Wearables
---

**Background:** The use of sensor-based movement analysis in clinical neurology is currently hindered by the scarcity of patient data, which restricts the development of robust, generalizable foundation models.

**Question / Future Work:** The transition from laboratory-based assessment to continuous, longitudinal real-world monitoring remains limited by the lack of personalized, clinically interpretable metrics that can account for inter-subject variability and track individual disease progression over time.

**Why It Matters:** Infrequent clinical rating scales are insensitive to subtle fluctuations; developing a longitudinal methodology for wearables is essential for clinical trial design and neurological patient management.

**Evidence:** Wearable digital biomarkers address the frequency problem, but population-level thresholds remain a blunt instrument for individual monitoring... prospective validation on longitudinal endpoints remains the subject of ongoing work.
