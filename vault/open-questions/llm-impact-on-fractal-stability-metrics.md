title: AI impact on fractal stability
---

**Background:** Large Language Model (LLM) based tools are increasingly used to generate and assist in software development tasks, fundamentally altering the nature of human commit activity.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unknown whether the observed long-range temporal correlations (quantified by the DFA scaling exponent α) in commit patterns remain a valid and reliable indicator of software product stability when development processes are heavily influenced by or driven by generative AI code generation. Future research is required to evaluate if the fractal structure persists as a meaningful quality metric in AI-augmented development environments.

**Why It Matters:** As generative AI becomes a standard tool in software engineering, the validity of traditional process-based quality metrics must be reassessed to determine if they still reflect the underlying architectural coherence or if they are decoupled from the quality of AI-generated outcomes.
