title: TTV dynamical mass-eccentricity degeneracy
---

**Background:** Multi-planet systems often exhibit transit timing variations (TTVs) in their transiting members, which are caused by gravitational interactions with other planetary companions. These interactions provide an indirect method to detect and characterize non-transiting planets, but rely on accurately sampling long-term orbital resonances and perturbations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to improve the observational sampling and dynamical modeling of TTVs to resolve mass-eccentricity degeneracies in compact multi-planet systems. Future work focuses on refining these orbital constraints to conclusively map the dynamical relationships between inner transiting planets and their outer perturbers.

**Why It Matters:** This is a significant bottleneck in exoplanetary characterization, as high-precision mass determination via dynamical modeling is essential for testing planet formation theories. Resolving these degeneracies is a recurring challenge in time-series analysis of planetary systems.
