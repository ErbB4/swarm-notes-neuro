title: Rigorous Proof for Ricci-CSAD Bridge
---

**Background:** The relationship between the discrete Ricci-curvature-based agent-graph metric and the macroscopic CSAD herding statistic has been established at a dominant-order scaling level under mean-field assumptions.

**Question / Future Work:** A rigorous mathematical proof establishing the mean-field bridge between the discrete agent-graph curvature metric and the classical CSAD (Cross-Sectional Absolute Dispersion) statistic remains unfinished. A fully rigorous theorem is required to propagate the mean-field convergence through the bipartite optimal-transport plan to provide formal validity to the interpretation of geometric signals.

**Why It Matters:** This theoretical link is essential for anchoring new geometric indicators to established, widely-used financial stability measures, providing formal validity to the interpretation of these new signals within the existing finance literature.

**Evidence:** We state this as a scaling identity because Step 2 of the derivation invokes a closed-form W1 between two near-degenerate kernels whose remainder bound we do not establish here; a fully rigorous proof is left to follow-on work.
