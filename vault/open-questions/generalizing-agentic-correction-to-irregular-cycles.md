title: Generalizing agentic correction to irregular cycles
---

**Background:** Agricultural yield forecasting models often lack inherent knowledge of crop phenology, leading to physically implausible predictions during dormant or non-growth phases. Post-hoc agentic correction can mitigate these errors by injecting domain-grounded logic, but performance is highly dependent on the model's ability to interpret structural time series patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining how to generalize phase-detection and refinement logic to crops without clear, periodic, or single-peak lifecycle patterns remains an open challenge. This involves designing mechanisms that remain robust when the structured seasonal assumptions inherent to existing agentic correction tools are violated.

**Why It Matters:** Expanding agentic correction to diverse agricultural production systems is critical for practical scalability beyond standard seasonal monocultures.
