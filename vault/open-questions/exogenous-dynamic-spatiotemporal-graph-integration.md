title: Exogenous and Dynamic Graphs
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal air quality forecasting models often rely on historical pollutant concentrations and static distance-based graph structures. The lack of integration with external environmental and anthropogenic factors such as meteorological conditions and traffic patterns limits model accuracy and physical interpretability.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to integrate domain-informed exogenous covariates (e.g., wind dynamics, traffic density) into graph convolutional forecasting and to develop dynamic graph structures that adapt to seasonal or temporal changes in pollutant transport mechanisms.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from purely univariate/historical-only graph models to multidimensional, dynamic spatiotemporal representations is essential for practical urban monitoring accuracy.
