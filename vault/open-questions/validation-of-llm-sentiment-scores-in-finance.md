title: Validating LLM Sentiment Metrics
---

**Background:** Large language model (LLM) sentiment scores lack standardized ground-truth validation in financial forecasting applications, raising questions about their reliability and impact on downstream predictive performance.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically evaluate the reliability of LLM-generated sentiment scores against established human-coded ground-truth datasets. Research should investigate how different prompt engineering strategies or model architectures affect the robustness of these sentiment metrics and whether refined sentiment extraction techniques can improve the performance of financial forecasting models compared to current approaches.

**Why It Matters:** The study identified the lack of robustness in LLM-generated sentiment scores as a potential reason for performance degradation, highlighting the need for rigorous validation methods to determine the actual utility of sentiment in financial modeling.

**Evidence:** Moreover, LLM-based sentiment scoring is not necessarily robust, introducing noise that could mislead downstream models, and for this work, we did not validate the LLM scores against human-coded ground truth.
