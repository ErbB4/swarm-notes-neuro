title: Expanding Feature Anchor Frameworks
---

**Background:** In human activity recognition (HAR), handcrafted time-series features (TSFs) are used for their efficiency and interpretability, whereas neural networks provide flexible but opaque latent representations. Recent architectures aim to bridge this gap by treating TSFs as explicit, modifiable feature anchors within the network.

**Question / Future Work:** Future development of anchor-based frameworks is needed to scale beyond specific sensor domains (e.g., IMUs) to complex physiological signals and to incorporate dynamic mechanisms for optimizing the feature anchor set based on task relevance or computational constraints.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the universality and efficiency of the anchor-based paradigm across broader sensor modalities and sparsity constraints is crucial for its adoption in real-world, resource-limited deployments.
