title: Privacy Risks of Shared Representations
---

**Background:** Sharing intermediate representations between distributed devices enables collaborative inference while mitigating the risk of raw data exposure. However, the degree of privacy protection offered by these representations against sophisticated reconstruction attacks remains an open question in federated learning and inference research.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to perform a comprehensive security and privacy analysis of shared intermediate representations and consensus embeddings against advanced reconstruction-based or feature-inference attacks. The robustness of these representations against broader classes of privacy-preserving attack models (e.g., differential privacy constraints or advanced inversion techniques) has not been rigorously characterized.

**Why It Matters:** Quantifying the privacy leakage risk of intermediate representations is fundamental to the deployment of federated inference in sensitive domains.
