title: Multi-site Generalization in Warning Systems
---

**Background:** Early warning systems for agriculture and epidemiology are typically developed and validated in local temporal case studies, hindering the assessment of their portability across varied environmental, geographic, and sensing configurations.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must determine how to achieve model transferability across diverse geographic regions and climate regimes, ensuring that learned predictive precursors are domain-general rather than artifacts of a specific microclimate or sensor deployment.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing cross-site transferability is a prerequisite for moving from localized proof-of-concept models to robust, widespread operational decision support systems.

**Evidence:** the analysis is based on a single-site temporal case study, so broader validation across additional vineyards, seasons, and sensing conditions is needed to assess generalizability.
