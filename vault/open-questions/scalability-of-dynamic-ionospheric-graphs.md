title: Scalability of Dynamic Ionospheric Graphs
---

**Background:** Dynamic graph modeling in spatio-temporal domains often relies on local station-pair data, making it unclear how performance scales to global, heterogeneous sensor networks.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must evaluate the scalability of dynamic graph forecasting to global GNSS networks, addressing the computational costs of dense graph message passing and the performance gap when moving from co-located receivers to widely distributed, heterogeneous monitoring stations across varying solar activity levels.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the gap between localized proof-of-concept and operational deployment for critical infrastructure space weather monitoring.

**Evidence:** Scaling to larger and more diverse observation networks is an immediate next step... Characterising this behaviour requires multi-station deployments with diverse spatial configurations, which we leave to future work.
