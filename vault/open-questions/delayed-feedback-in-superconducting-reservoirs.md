title: Delay in Josephson Reservoirs
---

**Background:** Physical reservoir computing often employs delay loops to improve memory and computational capacity. The efficacy and integration of such techniques into superconducting hardware remain largely unexplored.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research could explore the integration of external delayed feedback or input signals into Josephson junction reservoirs to determine if such modifications significantly enhance memory retention and prediction performance compared to solitary junction configurations.

**Why It Matters:** Delay loops are a standard performance-boosting technique in physical reservoir computing; understanding their utility in Josephson junction systems is critical for optimizing hardware-based neuromorphic architectures.
