title: Nascent State of AI Safety Evaluation Science
---

**Background:** The science of evaluating the safety, capabilities, and behavioral tendencies of advanced, autonomous AI systems is currently in its relative infancy.

**Question / Future Work:** Current AI evaluation methods, including benchmark testing and model red-teaming, struggle to provide sufficient assurance regarding the future safety of increasingly autonomous AGI systems. There is a lack of established, reliable methods to detect and prevent sophisticated failure modes, such as inner alignment risks, deceptive behavior, or the autonomous creation of self-preservation strategies. An urgent research program is needed to advance techniques like neural network interpretability and scalable oversight to ensure that advanced systems remain controllable and aligned with human objectives.

**Why It Matters:** Technical progress in AI capability is currently outpacing progress in the science of safety, creating a critical vulnerability where powerful systems could potentially bypass existing safeguards or act in ways that are fundamentally unaligned with human interests.
