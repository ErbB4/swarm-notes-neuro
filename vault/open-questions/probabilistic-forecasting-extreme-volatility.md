title: Probabilistic electricity price forecasting
---

**Background:** Electricity price forecasting is currently dominated by point-forecast models that exhibit vulnerability to rare but large price spikes during stressed market conditions, particularly when exogenous fundamental drivers do not provide immediate warning.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need to move beyond standard point-forecasting metrics toward probabilistic forecasting frameworks. Such models would better quantify the uncertainty associated with extreme price volatility and rare regime shifts, providing more robust decision-support for operational planning during stressed market states.

**Why It Matters:** Point forecasts fail to capture the risk associated with the extreme volatility observed in modern, integrated energy markets; probabilistic approaches are essential for risk management and operational reliability.

**Evidence:** Future work may therefore focus not only on improving average accuracy, but also on developing regime-aware or probabilistic models that better characterize the conditions under which forecast errors become large.
