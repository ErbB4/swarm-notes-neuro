title: Automated Exogenous Group Discovery
---

**Background:** The selection and integration of exogenous variables in forecasting models is often limited by reliance on expert-defined domain knowledge for grouping.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed into automated, data-driven group discovery mechanisms—such as attention-based clustering or learned sparsity patterns—to eliminate the need for manual site-specific configuration and improve model adaptability.

**Why It Matters:** Automated grouping is crucial for reducing engineering effort and improving the generalizability of forecasting models.

**Evidence:** the physical grouping prior, while effective, currently relies on domain knowledge to define the variable groups; automating this grouping through data-driven group discovery could improve applicability to domains where physical grouping is less obvious.
