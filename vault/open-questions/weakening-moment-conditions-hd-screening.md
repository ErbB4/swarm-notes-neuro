title: Weakening Moment Conditions for Screening
---

**Background:** Marginal screening procedures for high-dimensional models rely on specific physical dependence assumptions for time series and panel data.

**Question / Future Work:** The current theoretical framework assumes all moments of the random variables are finite to simplify asymptotic results. Extending these findings to weaker moment conditions, specifically for GARCH-type processes or other heavy-tailed time series, is a necessary future direction to broaden the practical applicability of these tests.

**Why It Matters:** Weakening these moment requirements is technically important to ensure the theory covers a wider array of financial and economic models where extreme observations are common.
