title: Probabilistic Epidemic Forecasting Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Public health decision-making necessitates an understanding of outcome uncertainty, yet existing epidemic forecasting benchmarks frequently prioritize deterministic point estimates over probabilistic outputs.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing benchmarking protocols that mandate probabilistic forecasting and uncertainty quantification is essential for translating epidemic model predictions into actionable risk assessments for policy.

**Why It Matters:** Decision-making under uncertainty is the primary application of epidemic modeling, requiring a shift in benchmark standards toward probabilistic evaluation.

**Evidence:** The current setup focuses on deterministic forecasting methods and evaluation metrics, without incorporating probabilistic forecasting or distribution-based evaluation strategies.
