title: True-KL0 Higher Dimensionality Limits
---

**Background:** Scored elicitation mechanisms, often used in AI oversight and forecasting, aim to align agent reporting incentives by rewarding submissions based on realized outcomes. The True-KL0 property, which provides a quantitative bound on misreporting gains, has been proved for specific parametric families of scoring rules in dimensions d ≤ 4.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unresolved whether the True-KL0 property can be established for higher-dimensional settings (d ≥ 5) or for broader families of scoring rules beyond the power-p pseudospherical family. Determining if similar M-isolating substitutions or alternative analytic strategies exist for these general cases is a major open problem for high-dimensional mechanism design.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the feasibility of True-KL0 in higher dimensions is critical for scaling AI oversight mechanisms as reporting interfaces grow in complexity.
