title: Multi-junction Reservoir Networks
---

**Background:** Neuromorphic systems often leverage coupled nonlinear oscillators to achieve richer dynamical behaviors than solitary units. The computational capacity and scaling potential of multi-junction superconducting networks for reservoir computing remain largely uncharacterized.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research should investigate the dynamics and computational performance of networks consisting of multiple coupled Josephson junctions, as these may offer increased complexity and superior nonlinear processing capabilities compared to single-junction reservoirs.

**Why It Matters:** Scaling superconducting reservoirs through coupled elements is essential for addressing more complex computational tasks and evaluating the collective processing power of superconducting neuromorphic hardware.
