title: Extending Noisy Network Framework
---

**Background:** The fluctuating dynamics of a complex network about a stable steady state can be described by linearized noisy dynamics. While this framework successfully characterizes both equilibrium and non-equilibrium steady states (NESSs) under various conditions, it remains limited to regimes where the system stays near a stable noise-free fixed point.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend the current linearized theoretical framework to handle complex network dynamics where the noise-free dynamics exhibit more intricate asymptotic behaviors, such as stable limit cycles, strange attractors, or multiple coexisting stable fixed points. Exploring these regimes is essential to understand non-equilibrium phase transitions and non-Gaussian behavior in steady-state distributions within a network context.

**Why It Matters:** Many real-world networks operate in regimes characterized by limit cycles or chaotic dynamics; extending this framework is essential for broader applicability in neuroscience, ecology, and other complex system domains.

**Evidence:** For instance, depending on the intrinsic dynamics (f_i), the connection matrix W, and the coupling function h(x_i,x_j), the asymptotic dynamics can lie on a stable limit cycle (periodic) or even chaotic (such as a strange attractor). ... It would be interesting to investigate systems with multiple and distinct steady states and examine the corresponding equilibrium or non-equilibrium phase transitions between these states.
