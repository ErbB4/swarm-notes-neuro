title: Early-Onset Offshore Infrastructure Detection
---

**Background:** The detection of offshore infrastructure currently relies on prior spatial localization, limiting the ability to identify new construction sites in their earliest stages. Current approaches often require a trade-off between high-precision detection models and high-recall background signal monitoring.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in detecting the earliest construction phases of offshore wind infrastructure without relying on fixed location priors or generating excessive false positives. Research is required to bridge the gap between spatial object detection and temporal anomaly monitoring to enable robust, near-real-time discovery of emerging infrastructure.

**Why It Matters:** Robust early-onset detection is essential for proactive monitoring of renewable energy deployment and requires moving beyond static site-based time series analysis.
