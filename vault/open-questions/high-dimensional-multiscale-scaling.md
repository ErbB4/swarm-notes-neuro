title: Scaling to higher-dimensional systems
---

**Background:** The identification of covariant Lyapunov vectors (CLVs) and the calculation of their angles serve as diagnostic tools for characterizing the stability of chaotic systems. However, the theoretical bridge connecting specific dynamical regimes, such as CLV tangencies, to the onset of extreme events in high-dimensional multiscale systems remains under investigation.

**Question / Future Work:** The framework needs to be expanded to accommodate more complex, higher-dimensional multiscale architectures to verify if the cascade of dynamical regimes and the proposed precursors hold as the number of degrees of freedom increases. Investigating this scaling behavior is essential for ensuring the robustness of extreme event forecasting in real-world systems.

**Why It Matters:** Confirming the applicability in high-dimensional multiscale systems is critical for practical utility and ensuring that the identified geometric mechanisms hold when the number of degrees of freedom significantly increases.
