title: Optimal EHR Representation Method
---

**Background:** The comparative efficacy of different Electronic Health Record (EHR) representation methods—specifically multivariate time-series, event streams, and textual event streams—remains subject to variation based on clinical context, data volume, and prediction task complexity.

**Question / Future Work:** While benchmark results indicate that event-stream models often provide robust performance and that specific representations excel in either ICU (high-frequency) or longitudinal (long-term/sparse) settings, there is no single universally optimal representation method. Further research is required to delineate the structural mechanisms that make specific representations superior for particular clinical trajectories, especially as data regimes move from few-shot to large-scale settings.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing which representation is most effective for specific clinical contexts is critical for developing reliable, scalable, and computationally efficient clinical decision support tools.
