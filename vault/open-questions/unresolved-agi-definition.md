title: Absence of Unified AGI Definition
---

**Background:** Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is expected by some to emerge within the next 3–15 years, yet there is no consensus on a unified, measurable definition of AGI across technical and policy communities.

**Question / Future Work:** The lack of a shared, universally accepted definition of AGI creates significant ambiguity in policy and research. It remains unclear which thresholds—such as performance on AI research tasks, scientific automation, broad cognitive automation, or physical work—should guide policy-making and monitor progress. Developing a standardized, empirically grounded framework is essential to clarify whether the industry is on the path toward AGI and to assess when critical capability milestones have been reached.

**Why It Matters:** Without a shared definition, policy makers cannot consistently evaluate progress, calibrate risk, or align on international governance frameworks, which is crucial for preparing for a potential AGI transition.
