title: Latent Inter-series Dependency Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Multivariate time series forecasting relies on modeling inter-series correlations, which often exhibit complex behaviors across various temporal scales and are prone to non-stationary evolution.

**Question / Future Work:** While current models utilize multi-level wavelet decomposition and adaptive graph learning, there remains a bottleneck in capturing latent inter-series associations that emerge at non-predefined temporal granularities. Future research needs to advance beyond standard static/dynamic fusion frameworks toward more fluid, latent dependency discovery mechanisms that are robust to high levels of non-stationarity.

**Why It Matters:** The effectiveness of multivariate forecasting is restricted by the rigidity of current graph structure learning methods, limiting the ability to adapt to complex, evolving, and latent inter-series associations.
