title: Extending MFRC to heterogeneous dimensions
---

**Background:** Reservoir computing is typically implemented for dynamical systems of a uniform dimensionality, simplifying the design of the input layer and reservoir-readout coupling.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending multifunctional reservoir computing (MFRC) schemes to emulate dynamical systems of varying state-space dimensions remains a significant, unresolved technical challenge. While padding techniques have been proposed as a possible solution, their effectiveness, robustness, and feasibility in maintaining the accuracy of the inferred dynamics across disparate dimensions have not been empirically verified.

**Why It Matters:** Real-world systems rarely exhibit uniform dimensionality; therefore, enabling cross-dimensional inference is essential for the generalization of MFRC to heterogeneous dynamical environments.

**Evidence:** Extending the proposed scheme to dynamical systems of different dimensions is another important issue that remains to be explored. One possible approach is to adopt the padding technique in data processing, yet the feasibility of this strategy remains to be investigated.
