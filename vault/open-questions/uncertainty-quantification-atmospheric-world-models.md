title: Uncertainty in Atmospheric World Models
---

**Background:** Atmospheric modeling frameworks often rely on predefined grid structures, which limits the integration of heterogeneous, raw observational data and necessitates complex data assimilation procedures. Atmospheric world models aim to move toward observation-native, grid-free paradigms to better utilize multi-source sensor inputs.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should address the lack of explicit uncertainty quantification in current observation-native world models. Specifically, developing frameworks that incorporate uncertainty into the latent observation space, employing ensemble forecasting techniques, and utilizing probabilistic modeling for downstream applications are essential for enhancing robustness and reliability in meteorological predictions.

**Why It Matters:** Quantifying uncertainty is critical for high-stakes atmospheric forecasting and decision-making, particularly in extreme weather events where model confidence can vary significantly across space and time.
