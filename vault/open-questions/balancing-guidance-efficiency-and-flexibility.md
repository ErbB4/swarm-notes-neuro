title: Efficiency-Flexible Guidance Balance
---

**Background:** Generative data assimilation frameworks face a fundamental tension between the inference-time flexibility of likelihood-based guidance and the computational efficiency of short reverse-time sampling chains.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methods, such as consistency models or stochastic interpolants, that preserve the adaptability of likelihood-based guidance without necessitating large reverse-step budgets that increase computational latency in cycling assimilation.

**Why It Matters:** Achieving this balance is vital for making diffusion-based data assimilation computationally competitive with traditional ensemble methods in real-time, high-dimensional cycling applications.
