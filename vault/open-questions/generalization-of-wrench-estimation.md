title: Generalization of wrench estimation
---

**Background:** Data-driven wrench estimation in robotic systems currently lacks established methods for handling high-frequency vibrations encountered in rapid, non-periodic tasks like industrial grinding. Current research is limited by a lack of diverse, high-quality, real-world datasets that capture these transient and unstructured wrench fluctuations across different robotic platforms.

**Question / Future Work:** The authors highlight that the current validation of sensorless wrench estimation is limited to a single hydraulic manipulator and grinding setting. Future work is required to evaluate the generalization of the proposed Frequency-aware Decomposition Network across a broader range of contact objects, robot platforms, and diverse manipulation tasks.

**Why It Matters:** Robustness and cross-platform generalization are critical bottlenecks for the deployment of sensorless wrench estimation in real-world industrial environments where dynamics vary significantly.
