title: DLPFC Statistical Learning Modulation Mechanism
---

**Background:** The dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC) is implicated in both executive control and the regulation of implicit, unsupervised statistical learning, yet the precise mechanisms through which these processes interact remain unclear.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining whether DLPFC-mediated modulation of statistical learning reflects a strategic shift in information sampling (e.g., exploration-exploitation trade-offs) or a neurophysiological gating mechanism that constrains implicit learning is necessary to understand the interaction between cognitive control and unconscious acquisition of regularities.

**Why It Matters:** Clarifying this mechanism is fundamental to reconciling the role of prefrontal executive control with autonomous, unsupervised learning systems.

**Evidence:** As an alternative, we propose that right DLPFC inhibition shifts cognitive processing toward a more exploratory information-sampling strategy, a view supported by our finding of significantly greater reaction time variability in the right and bilateral stimulation groups.
