title: Joint Causal Discovery Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Causal discovery methods rely on statistical assumptions to infer directed relationships from time-series data, often struggling with noise, limited sample sizes, and unobserved confounders in real-world industrial systems.

**Question / Future Work:** The reliance on pre-learned causal graphs poses a fundamental limitation for deploying causally-constrained models in dynamic or non-stationary environments. Investigating end-to-end joint learning of causal structures alongside task-specific objectives remains a critical bottleneck for robust, autonomous time-series analysis.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses the core tension between static causal priors and dynamic real-world environments, which is essential for advancing robust, interpretable forecasting.
