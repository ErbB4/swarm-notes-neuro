title: Latent Pseudo-Anomaly Diversity Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Time-series anomaly detection often relies on generating pseudo-anomalies to train robust models, but current methods frequently produce synthetic data with insufficient diversity.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop mechanisms for generating high-diversity pseudo-anomalies in latent spaces, as current perturbation methods often yield limited variants that fail to capture the full spectrum of complex, real-world anomalies.

**Why It Matters:** The robustness of unsupervised anomaly classifiers is intrinsically linked to the coverage of the synthetic anomaly distribution; improving this is central to model performance and generalization.
