title: Mitigating VLM Rationalization Traps
---

**Background:** Vision-Language Models often produce plausible-sounding but factually incorrect justifications for decisions in high-stakes visual tasks. This phenomenon, where the model generates speculative narratives to explain away artifacts, poses a challenge for reliable forensic verification.

**Question / Future Work:** How can kinematic hallucinations and post-hoc narrative justifications in VLMs be mitigated to ensure reasoning is grounded in factual visual features rather than speculative interpretations? Identifying methods to decouple generative reasoning from classification accuracy in high-stakes domains is a key research priority.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for building reliable forensic and diagnostic AI systems where accurate and interpretable reasoning is a prerequisite for human-in-the-loop verification.
