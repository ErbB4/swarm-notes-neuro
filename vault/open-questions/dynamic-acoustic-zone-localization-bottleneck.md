title: Dynamic Acoustic Zone Localization
---

**Background:** Current acoustic localization models often assume static conversational participants and predefined group counts. Predicting user intent in real-world environments requires accounting for dynamic shifts in participants and varying individual behavioral patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing robust mechanisms for conversation partner localization in highly dynamic social scenarios where participants enter, leave, or move, moving beyond fixed-count classification tasks.

**Why It Matters:** This is the primary constraint preventing wearable-based audio enhancement from being deployed in unstructured, real-world social environments.
