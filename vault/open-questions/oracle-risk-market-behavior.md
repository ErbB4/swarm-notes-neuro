title: Trading under oracle risk
---

**Background:** Decentralized prediction markets rely on oracles to resolve real-world outcomes, but the resolution process often involves disputes that can impact market integrity and participant behavior. The dynamics of how traders respond to markets under active oracle dispute remain inadequately understood.

**Question / Future Work:** While the study identifies a two-stage response pattern where traders re-enter wrong-case markets shortly after an oracle risk anchor, further research is needed to determine the causal drivers of this behavior and how such post-anchor trading activity affects the final resolution accuracy or potential market manipulation.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding oracle-risk market dynamics is critical for the robustness of decentralized oracle designs and for preventing information distortion in markets that depend on external settlement.
