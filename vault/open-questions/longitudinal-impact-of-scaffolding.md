title: Longitudinal Impact of Scaffolding
---

**Background:** Proactive adaptive systems in education are increasingly designed to provide real-time scaffolding for specific tasks. However, the long-term impact of these interventions on student meta-cognitive development remains understudied.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research needs to evaluate whether prolonged interaction with forecast-driven collaborative scaffolds fosters durable self-regulation and independent metacognitive skills. This requires longitudinal studies to determine if the support eventually fades and whether students internalize the regulatory mechanisms provided by the AI tutor.

**Why It Matters:** Medium; understanding the long-term impact is critical for ensuring that AI tutors promote sustainable learning outcomes rather than creating dependency.

**Evidence:** Longitudinal studies could clarify whether repeated exposure to proactive collaborative scaffolding supports durable self-regulation and metacognitive awareness.
