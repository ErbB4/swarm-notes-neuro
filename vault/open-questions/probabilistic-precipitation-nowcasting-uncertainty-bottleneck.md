title: Probabilistic Nowcasting Uncertainty Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Precipitation nowcasting is inherently stochastic, yet many state-of-the-art architectures remain deterministic, limiting their reliability for decision-making in high-impact weather scenarios.

**Question / Future Work:** The need to develop methods for quantifying uncertainty in high-resolution, long-horizon precipitation nowcasting without sacrificing the computational efficiency of modern state-space-based frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Deterministic point estimates in chaotic systems often miss the multi-modal nature of extreme weather, making uncertainty quantification a vital next step for operational adoption.

**Evidence:** the current framework is deterministic and does not quantify forecast uncertainty.
