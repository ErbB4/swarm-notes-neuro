title: Multivariate Mechanistic Outbreak Forecasting
---

**Background:** Epidemic forecasting models frequently focus on isolated, univariate time series, often failing to account for multi-wave dynamics driven by exogenous factors like policy changes or spatial interactions.

**Question / Future Work:** Advancing epidemic forecasting requires frameworks that integrate multivariate signals, spatial coupling, and mechanistic biological constraints (such as population susceptibility or transmission dynamics) rather than treating outbreaks as independent, isolated univariate events.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between empirical univariate benchmarks and real-world, multivariate, and mechanistically-informed forecasting is a central challenge for future outbreak response.

**Evidence:** We acknowledge that such an approach inherently neglects multi-wave dynamics... We also focus on univariate forecasting at the level of single location, and disease outcomes.
