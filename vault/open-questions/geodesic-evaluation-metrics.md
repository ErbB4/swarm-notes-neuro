title: Geodesic-based Forecast Evaluation Metrics
---

**Background:** Forecast reconciliation methods on nonlinear manifolds are typically evaluated using Euclidean-based scoring rules, which may not capture the geometry of the constraint space.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop proper scoring rules that are explicitly geodesic-distance-based or manifold-aware to provide theoretically valid evaluation of reconciled distributions on nonlinear coherent manifolds.

**Why It Matters:** Misalignment between the evaluation metric and the underlying geometry of the forecast space can lead to inaccurate performance assessment and suboptimal model selection.
