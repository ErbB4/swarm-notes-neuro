title: SDN Controller Placement Trade-offs
---

**Background:** The placement of a central controller in software-defined satellite networking involves a trade-off between global network visibility and the computational limitations of space-borne hardware.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal placement for the central controller in large-scale non-terrestrial networks remains an unresolved challenge, as ground-based controllers face connectivity gaps while satellite-based controllers are restricted by Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) and propagation latency. Research is needed to identify architectures—such as hybrid space-ground or split-control paradigms—that effectively balance global orchestration requirements with the physical constraints of orbital environments.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical architectural bottleneck for scaling SDN to massive, highly dynamic constellations, as it directly impacts control latency and network robustness.
