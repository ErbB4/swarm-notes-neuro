title: Spaceborne Remote Sensing Adaptation
---

**Background:** The shift from in situ hyperspectral sensors to spaceborne imaging spectroscopy offers a potential for global monitoring, yet this transition introduces complex observational errors related to atmospheric interference and sensor noise. Evaluating whether physics-aware models can maintain performance under these degradation factors is a critical open research direction.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must evaluate the performance of meta-learning retrieval frameworks under spaceborne conditions, specifically addressing challenges such as atmospheric correction uncertainties, adjacency effects, and sensor-specific spectral noise. Bridging this gap is critical for moving from local, in situ monitoring to operational, large-scale spaceborne applications.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between ground-based and satellite-based remote sensing is essential for achieving operational aquatic ecosystem monitoring.
