title: Thermodynamics of Noisy Networks
---

**Background:** Thermodynamic variables such as heat, work, and entropy production provide a foundation for describing non-equilibrium systems. When network dynamics are treated as stochastic processes, these quantities can potentially be derived from the network's structural properties and observable data like time-correlation functions.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to systematically derive the energetics (heat, work, and entropy production rates) of general linearized noisy networks at non-equilibrium steady states (NESSs) directly from network structure and measurable statistical quantities. This would allow for a deeper understanding of how local nodal dynamics and global connectivity contribute to the thermodynamic cost of maintaining a steady state in a network.

**Why It Matters:** Linking the statistical properties of network dynamics to rigorous thermodynamic concepts like entropy production is fundamental to the study of active matter, biological signaling, and metabolic network regulation.

**Evidence:** one can further identify the associated energetics, such as heat, work, and entropy production rates for general linearized noisy networks at NESSs, one can then theoretically derive the network energetics in terms of the network structure properties and other measurable quantities.
