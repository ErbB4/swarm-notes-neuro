title: Industrial DSL Expressivity Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Data preparation for industrial cyber-physical systems often relies on ad hoc, one-off scripts, which lack maintainability and reusability. Expanding these systems to support domain-specific data types and diverse source formats is a recognized necessity for broader applicability.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work aims to evolve DSLs for industrial data to natively handle domain-specific units (e.g., electrical quantities) and diverse file formats (e.g., HDF5, TSDBs), moving beyond simple string/numeric processing. This addresses the bottleneck of limited expressiveness that restricts the automation of complex domain-centric data workflows.

**Why It Matters:** These features address the bottleneck of limited expressiveness and format compatibility, which currently restricts the tool from being used across a wider range of industrial data-centric workflows.
