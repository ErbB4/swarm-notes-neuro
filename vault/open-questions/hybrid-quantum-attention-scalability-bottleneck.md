title: Hybrid Quantum Attention Scalability
---

**Background:** Hybrid quantum-classical attention modules are limited by the computational requirements and noisy gate operations of current variational quantum circuits.

**Question / Future Work:** The practical scalability of QASA and similar hybrid mechanisms remains unproven as the sequence length, input feature dimension, and circuit depth increase, particularly in high-latency financial trading environments. Investigating the compute-performance trade-off for these modules beyond small-scale experimental benchmarks is a critical requirement.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the scaling limits is essential for determining if quantum-enhanced financial modules can transition from experimental prototypes to real-world, high-frequency decentralized finance applications.

**Evidence:** Complexity/efficiency analysis ranks ensembles most cost-effective; QASA-Hybrid provides a balanced mid-cost option; QASA-Sequence trades compute for peak Sharpe.
