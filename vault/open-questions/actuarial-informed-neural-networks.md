title: Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks
---

**Background:** Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks involve integrating domain-specific constraints directly into the loss functions of neural models.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should explore the development of Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks, analogous to Physics-Informed Neural Networks, where regulatory and biological constraints—such as Li-Lee coherence, Gompertzian monotonicity, and stationarity conditions—are embedded directly into the training objective rather than being verified post-hoc.

**Why It Matters:** Integrating constraints directly into the learning process would improve the structural consistency of neural models, potentially reducing the need for post-hoc corrections and increasing regulatory trust in internal risk models.

**Evidence:** A natural next step is the development of Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks, analogous to Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), where actuarial constraints, such as Li-Lee coherence, Gompertzian monotonicity, and stationarity conditions, are embedded directly in the training loss function rather than verified post-hoc.
