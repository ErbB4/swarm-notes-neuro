title: Modeling Subjectivity in Trajectories
---

**Background:** Trajectory prediction models often struggle to explicitly learn and incorporate the subjective, anisotropic behaviors that influence individual agent movement.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing more robust methods to explicitly represent, learn, and modulate trajectory predictions based on the unique, time-varying subjective intentions or 'biases' of agents remains a significant challenge for human-like social navigation. Future work is needed to refine the integration of these inferred subjectivities to ensure they provide stable and high-fidelity conditioning for complex multi-agent forecasting tasks.

**Why It Matters:** This represents a fundamental bottleneck in achieving human-like predictability in complex social navigation scenarios, where agent subjectivity is a primary driver of anisotropic behavior.

**Evidence:** Despite great efforts, it remains difficult to explicitly learn and forecast these subjectivities, let alone further modulate models’ predictions through a specific ego’s subjectivity.
