title: Galaxy Velocity Modeling Ansatz Validity
---

**Background:** The assumption that galaxy velocities follow the CDM plus baryon field velocity rather than the total matter field velocity is frequently used to simplify large-scale structure calculations in the presence of massive neutrinos.

**Question / Future Work:** A self-consistent theoretical derivation is required to validate the approximation that galaxy velocities equal the velocity of the cold dark matter plus baryon field. Further investigation is needed to quantify potential deviations from this ansatz and assess its robustness in future massive neutrino cosmology models.

**Why It Matters:** This approximation is central to modeling redshift-space distortions in massive neutrino cosmologies, and its validity directly impacts the robustness of neutrino mass constraints derived from current and future galaxy surveys.

**Evidence:** While a self consistent theoretical derivation of this result is still the subject of ongoing study, we adopt this ansatz in all that follows. We plan to investigate possible deviations from this approximation in a forthcoming work.
