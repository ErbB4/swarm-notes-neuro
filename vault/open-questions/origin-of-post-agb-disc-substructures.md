title: Origins of inner disc substructures
---

**Background:** The inner rims of post-AGB circumbinary discs are often resolved using NIR interferometry, but the morphological complexity and presence of substructures within these rims remain poorly constrained. Understanding whether these substructures are long-lived or follow the binary orbit is crucial to determining if they are driven by thermal responses to stellar radiation, dynamical binary interactions, or hydrodynamical instabilities.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine the physical origin and dynamical evolution of azimuthal brightness enhancements and substructures in the inner rims of post-AGB circumbinary discs. Specifically, future work is needed to perform multi-epoch, time-resolved imaging to distinguish between features that follow the binary orbital motion versus those that evolve on longer timescales or orbit at local Keplerian velocities.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck in understanding the interaction between post-AGB binaries and their circumbinary discs, directly impacting models of mass depletion, binary evolution, and potentially second-generation planet formation.
