title: Mechanisms of anti-solar rotation
---

**Background:** Stellar differential rotation in cool, active stars has been observed to exhibit both solar-like and anti-solar behaviors, but the underlying mechanisms remain poorly understood.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for additional, systematic detections of anti-solar surface differential rotation in diverse star populations to clarify the physical mechanisms—such as the role of meridional flow or specific stellar parameter regimes—that lead to this rotational behavior. Establishing a robust observational census of anti-solar shear is necessary to constrain and refine current stellar magnetic dynamo models.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the divergence between solar-like and anti-solar differential rotation is a fundamental problem in stellar astrophysics and dynamo theory.

**Evidence:** However, the samples are still insufficient so far, and more detections of anti-solar differential rotation are required so as to clarify its mechanism.
