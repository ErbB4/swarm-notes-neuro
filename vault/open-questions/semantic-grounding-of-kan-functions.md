title: Semantic grounding of KAN functions
---

**Background:** While Kolmorogov-Arnold Networks offer inherent interpretability through learnable edge functions, these functions are often mathematically complex and may not naturally align with human-understandable concepts.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to map raw, latent-space KAN edge functions to human-interpretable semantic concepts, potentially through techniques like concept activation vectors or disentangled representation learning, to improve the utility of mechanistic explanations in safety-critical time-series forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between mathematical transparency and human semantic understanding is essential for the practical adoption of inherently interpretable models in fields where stakeholders must trust and act upon machine-generated forecasts.
