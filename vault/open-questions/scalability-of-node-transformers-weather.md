title: Scalability of NODE-Transformers in Weather Forecasting
---

**Background:** Weather forecasting models are increasingly exploring the integration of Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (NODEs) into transformer-based architectures to model continuous latent dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear whether the accuracy and physical consistency gains of continuous-depth NODE-augmented transformers scale to high-resolution deployments or hold up against current large-scale pre-trained foundation models. Future work is required to investigate the computational overhead and stability of these continuous architectures in high-fidelity settings.

**Why It Matters:** Evaluating the scalability and robustness of these models is essential for determining their viability as operational replacements for traditional numerical weather prediction.
