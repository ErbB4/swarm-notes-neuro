title: Clinical Utility vs Benchmarking
---

**Background:** Clinical decision support systems require accurate interpretation of longitudinal electronic health records, yet current models often struggle with quantitative trend hallucination and non-uniform temporal data. COTCAgent mitigates this by decoupling statistical computation from language generation using a structured knowledge base.

**Question / Future Work:** The gap between algorithmic performance on structured benchmark rankings and actual clinical utility—including clinician-verified risk assessment and reliability—remains a major bottleneck for safe deployment. Future work must bridge this by characterizing failure modes and aligning intermediate reasoning artifacts with expert clinical validation.

**Why It Matters:** The distinction between benchmark performance and real-world clinical reliability is a critical, unresolved obstacle for the adoption of medical foundation models.
