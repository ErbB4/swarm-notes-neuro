title: Identifying Predictive Exogenous Signals
---

**Background:** Forecasting models in healthcare often struggle to distinguish between informative indicators and noisy, non-predictive exogenous context during periods of system disruption.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear how to systematically identify and validate which exogenous signals possess true predictive utility for hospitalization outcomes, as opposed to signals that contribute noise and instability to forecasting models. Further research is required to characterize the causal or meaningful dependencies of these signals to improve the reliability of decision-support systems.

**Why It Matters:** Improving forecasting reliability in healthcare requires distinguishing truly predictive context from noise; otherwise, models risk poor generalization and unstable decision support during critical events.

**Evidence:** an important direction for future work is identifying which exogenous signals directly influence hospitalization outcomes. Understanding which signals meaningfully precede changes in demand can help narrow the factors that hospitals should prioritize.
