title: Refining extragalactic radio background models
---

**Background:** The extragalactic radio background (ERB) is a critical component for estimating the stimulated decay of axion-like particles in cosmological intensity mapping, but its precise amplitude and spectral shape at different redshifts remain subject to significant model uncertainties.

**Question / Future Work:** While empirical relations between star formation rates, stellar masses, and radio luminosity exist, extrapolating these to high redshifts introduces uncertainty in the calculated ERB intensity. Further refinement of galaxy evolution models and radio luminosity functions is required to improve the accuracy of sensitivity forecasts for axion-like particle dark matter decay.

**Why It Matters:** The ERB intensity directly determines the stimulated decay rate, which is the primary signal being probed; therefore, model uncertainties in the ERB propagate directly into uncertainties in the constraints on the axion-photon coupling parameter.
