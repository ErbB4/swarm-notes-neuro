title: Generalizing High-Speed Racing Perception
---

**Background:** High-speed autonomous racing requires robust perception systems that function reliably despite significant domain shifts from conventional urban driving datasets. While cross-domain transfer techniques exist, their effectiveness remains limited by the small size of real-world racing datasets and the fundamental differences in dynamic complexity between urban and high-speed environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in bridging the perception domain gap between synthetic and real-world high-speed racing data, particularly due to the scarcity of high-quality real-world labels. Future work is required to develop domain adaptation techniques tailored for extreme high-speed dynamics and to improve joint detection-prediction performance across disparate motion-distribution domains.

**Why It Matters:** This question highlights the critical bottleneck in high-speed perception, where limited real-world data and sim-to-real gaps hinder model generalization.

**Evidence:** Future work will investigate domain adaptation techniques tailored to high-speed racing, joint detection–prediction modeling, and multi-agent interaction reasoning.
