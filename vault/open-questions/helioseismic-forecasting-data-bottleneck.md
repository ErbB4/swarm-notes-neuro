title: Helioseismic Forecasting Data Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Solar acoustic oscillations (p-modes) show frequency shifts that correlate with long-term solar magnetic activity cycles, but the exact physical mechanisms—whether magnetic or thermal—remain under debate. Developing robust machine-learning forecasting models for these shifts is currently limited by the length of available high-quality observational datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need to improve machine-learning-based forecasting methods for p-mode frequency shifts, particularly to better capture short-term periodicities like quasi-biennial oscillations. Future work requires the integration of diverse, longer-term helioseismic datasets and additional solar activity indices to move beyond nascent, limited-data forecasting approaches.

**Why It Matters:** The lack of long-term, high-resolution helioseismic data prevents the full validation of machine learning models for solar cycle prediction, which is essential for bridging the gap between solar interior dynamics and space weather forecasting.
