title: Fisher information in QRC expressivity
---

**Background:** Quantum reservoir computing architectures rely on feedback mechanisms and quantum state inputs to perform time-series forecasting. The performance and adaptability of these systems are strongly influenced by the specific quantum correlations present in the input states.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to quantify the precise relationship between the quantum Fisher information of the reservoir's input states and the system's sensitivity to parameter variations, as a way to formally explain the observed improvements in nonlinear computational expressivity when using multiphoton, indistinguishable states.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing this formal link would provide a theoretical foundation for optimizing photonic quantum reservoir designs beyond empirical trial-and-error, potentially identifying fundamental bounds on their expressive capacity.

**Evidence:** The observed performance gain can be attributed to the combination of multiphoton inputs and adaptive dynamical feedback, which increases the reservoir’s sensitivity to variations in the parameters encoding of the input sequence. Such increased sensitivity can be interpreted in terms of higher Fisher Information associated with indistinguishable two-photon states.
