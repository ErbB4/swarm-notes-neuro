title: Multi-scale Rhythm Interaction Modelling
---

**Background:** Seizure prediction models often rely on linear time-series representations, which may fail to capture the complex, rhythmic nature of biological processes influencing seizure likelihood.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop and validate multi-scale phase-aware risk models that integrate both circadian and multi-day rhythms to better understand the temporal dependencies of seizure occurrence. Research should address whether seizure likelihood is specifically modulated by combinations of these rhythms, rather than isolated oscillations.

**Why It Matters:** Current models often miss the multi-scale interactions that likely characterize the underlying physiological processes of epilepsy, limiting the accuracy of seizure forecasting systems.

**Evidence:** Future work should examine whether seizure likelihood is specifically modulated by combinations of these rhythms, rather than isolated oscillations.
