title: Generalizability of Proximity Semantics
---

**Background:** Proximity semantics characterizes deictic disambiguation where meaning is established through spatial, temporal, and visual closeness of multimodal elements in a shared interaction space. The generalizability and formal limits of this framework remain under-explored outside of initial design probes.

**Question / Future Work:** The generalizability of the proximity semantics (PS) framework across varied data types, complex multi-user environments, and differing cognitive loads needs to be rigorously evaluated to determine its utility as a universal design principle for multimodal analytical interfaces.

**Why It Matters:** Validating PS as a robust analytical lens is necessary for designing consistent, user-centric query interfaces that transcend individual experimental platforms.

**Evidence:** Although PS represents a bounded interaction space, our findings suggest that spatial, temporal, and syntactic forms of proximity are meaningful mechanisms through which users express analytical intent.
