title: Adaptive Spatiotemporal Forecasting Scalability
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal forecasting systems are often constrained by the trade-off between architectural complexity and the ability to model dynamic, non-local spatial correlations. Current state-of-the-art models frequently rely on static graph definitions or struggle to integrate heterogeneous contextual signals in large-scale environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop robust spatiotemporal forecasting systems that move beyond static graphs, incorporating adaptive spatial structure modeling and multitask capabilities to handle the heterogeneity and dynamism of real-world urban traffic systems.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the fundamental transition from controlled benchmark forecasting to reliable deployment in complex, evolving urban infrastructure.
