title: GP Multivariate TSC Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Genetic programming (GP) is increasingly applied to automate feature learning for time series classification (TSC). Existing GP-based approaches for TSC often focus on univariate series, limiting their applicability to more complex, multi-sensor environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should extend GP-based TSC frameworks to handle multivariate time series, where cross-variate dependencies provide critical discriminative information. This requires developing and evaluating specialized program structures and operators capable of capturing inter-variate correlations within an evolutionary search.

**Why It Matters:** Multivariate time series are standard in real-world industrial and clinical applications; overcoming the univariate bottleneck is essential for the practical utility of evolutionary feature learning.
