title: Multivariate Polyspectral Framework Bottleneck
---

**Background:** The identification of genuine high-order neural interactions is confounded by linear instantaneous mixing, such as volume conduction, which masks underlying nonlinear dependencies in sensor-level data.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending existing antisymmetric polyspectral frameworks to multivariate and multidimensional signal scenarios, where multiple interacting source regions are defined by vectorial components, remains an open computational and theoretical challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Advancing from univariate-frequency-centric analysis to full multivariate/multidimensional source-level connectivity is essential for high-resolution neuroimaging and complex sensor array processing.

**Evidence:** Specifically, there is no established infrastructure to investigate complex nonlinear couplings among N interacting regions (multivariate) where each source is intrinsically vectorial (multidimensional).
