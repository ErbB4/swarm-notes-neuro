title: Limits of Synthetic Training
---

**Background:** Anomaly detection models often rely on rule-based synthetic data generation to avoid the need for labeled real-world anomalous segments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must address how synthetic data generation can transcend simple rule-based perturbations to capture the diverse, subtle, and context-dependent failure modes that occur in real-world complex systems.

**Why It Matters:** The limitation of synthetic data coverage remains the primary obstacle to deploying these systems in uncontrolled, highly variable physical environments.
