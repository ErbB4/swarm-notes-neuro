title: Extended Air Traffic Forecasting Horizons
---

**Background:** Short-term air traffic flow prediction in terminal airspace has traditionally been performed over short time horizons using aggregated historical traffic data. Extending predictive models to longer time horizons remains a critical objective for advanced air traffic management.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research needs to evaluate the effectiveness of microscopic state-to-flow modeling frameworks when applied to extended prediction horizons (e.g., 30 or 90 minutes) to determine if performance benefits persist as uncertainty grows.

**Why It Matters:** The current evaluation is limited to a 15-minute horizon; confirming performance at longer horizons is essential for strategic air traffic flow management.

**Evidence:** Extending the framework to longer forecasting horizons, such as 30-minute-ahead and 90-minute-ahead prediction tasks is both natural and an important direction for future research.
