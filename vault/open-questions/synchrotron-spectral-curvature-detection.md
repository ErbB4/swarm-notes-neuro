title: Detecting synchrotron spectral curvature
---

**Background:** Synchrotron emission is commonly approximated by a power-law, yet physical processes like electron aging and the superposition of components lead to spectral curvature, which remains poorly constrained by current sensitivity.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining whether observed synchrotron spectra deviate from a power law requires more precise measurement of curvature parameters, especially in low-signal regions where current instrumental sensitivities and frequency coverage are insufficient to break degeneracies between the spectral index and the curvature.

**Why It Matters:** Reliably detecting spectral curvature is fundamental for minimizing foreground contamination in future CMB B-mode polarization experiments.

**Evidence:** Detecting synchrotron curvature on a pixel-by-pixel basis is limited not only by sensitivity but also by the frequency leverage of the current data.
