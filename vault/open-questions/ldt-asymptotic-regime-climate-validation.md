title: LDT Asymptotic Regime Validation in Climate Systems
---

**Background:** Large deviation theory (LDT) provides a framework for analyzing the probability of rare events in time-averaged dynamical systems, but its application to complex Earth systems often assumes stationarity. The validity and empirical convergence of LDT in the context of non-stationary, multi-scale climate dynamics remain open challenges that require further verification.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to determine whether the asymptotic regime required for the Large Deviation Principle (LDP) is genuinely reached in empirical climate datasets. The existing framework faces challenges in balancing block lengths and data density to accurately capture tail behavior without smoothing out significant rare events, necessitating more robust empirical validation of the LDP under varying climate conditions.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental methodological limitation for applying statistical physics principles like LDT to the Earth system. If the LDP asymptotic assumptions are not met, the resulting return time predictions may be systematically biased, affecting climate risk assessment.
