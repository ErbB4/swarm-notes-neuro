title: Causal Identification of Dominance
---

**Background:** Causal identification of the relative contributions of individual capabilities and environmental factors to outcome variance is essential to definitively separate inherent talent from structural opportunity. The current reliance on observational variance decompositions limits the ability to isolate the proportion of outcome disparity attributable to individual-specific capabilities versus environmental conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of causal-mobility research designs is required to move beyond purely observational variance decompositions, enabling a more precise isolation of environmental dominance from individual-specific capability effects and sorting/selection biases.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental methodological bottleneck for verifying the strength of the environmental-dominance inequality against capability-based explanations.
