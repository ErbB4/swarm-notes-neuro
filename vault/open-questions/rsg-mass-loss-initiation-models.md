title: RSG mass-loss initiation mechanisms
---

**Background:** While the pulsation and convective processes in AGB stars are well-described by existing models, the mechanisms triggering mass loss in red supergiants (RSGs) remain under-constrained due to the complex, irregular nature of their variability.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for 3D radiation-hydrodynamic (RHD) models for red supergiants (RSGs) that incorporate wavelength-dependent opacities and can accurately reproduce observed atmospheric extensions, pulsation mode switching, and extreme episodic mass-loss events, as current models are primarily optimized for lower-mass AGB stars and often struggle to capture the full physics of RSG mass-loss initiation.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this is critical for understanding the final evolutionary stages of massive stars and their contribution to the chemical enrichment of the interstellar medium, as the current mismatch between observations and models limits the ability to predict pre-supernova mass loss.
