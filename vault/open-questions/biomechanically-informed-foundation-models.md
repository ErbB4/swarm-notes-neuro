title: Biomechanical Interpretation of Models
---

**Background:** Time series foundation models have been demonstrated to successfully replace recorded robotic reaching trials with synthetic ones, thereby reducing assessment duration while maintaining clinical reliability. However, these models operate as black boxes, providing accurate aggregate kinematic statistics without offering insights into the underlying biomechanical strategies employed by individuals.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should investigate integrating biomechanically constrained decoders or musculoskeletal simulators with foundation models to move beyond purely data-driven, black-box predictions toward more interpretable, physiologically grounded simulations of movement.

**Why It Matters:** Improving model interpretability is critical for clinical acceptance and understanding the pathological mechanisms of motor impairment following stroke, beyond mere statistical performance.
