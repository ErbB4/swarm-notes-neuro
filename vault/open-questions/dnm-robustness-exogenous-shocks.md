title: DNM Robustness Against Exogenous Shocks
---

**Background:** Dynamical Network Marker (DNM) theory identifies precursors to critical transitions in high-dimensional systems by detecting specific interacting elements that exhibit characteristic behaviors such as increased fluctuations. While successfully applied to some financial datasets, the theory assumes a closed system with limited noise, which diverges from the reality of open financial markets subject to frequent, heterogeneous exogenous shocks.

**Question / Future Work:** A primary open challenge is the development of robust DNM-based early-warning indicators that can effectively account for and remain resilient against exogenous shocks and external information that influence trading behavior but are not captured within the closed-system dynamics of the DNM framework. Current approaches, such as moving average smoothing, are insufficient to fully decouple these external influences from the internal indicators of structural instability.

**Why It Matters:** Without addressing the impact of exogenous shocks, the practical applicability of DNM for real-time risk management remains limited, as external market noise frequently triggers false positives or obscures pre-instability signals.

**Evidence:** Real financial markets are open systems subject to continual external shocks, deviating from the DNM assumption of closed interactions among participants with limited random noise.
