title: Improved Solar Wind Thermodynamics
---

**Background:** Solar wind models typically rely on polytropic or simplified thermodynamic treatments to reduce computational expense, which limits their ability to capture realistic plasma heating and cooling mechanisms.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to incorporate more sophisticated and physically realistic thermodynamic processes, such as thermal conduction, radiative cooling, and heating terms, into global magnetohydrodynamic models to improve the accuracy of simulated background solar wind properties.

**Why It Matters:** Thermodynamics is crucial for correctly modeling solar wind speed, density, and temperature profiles, which significantly influence the propagation speed and morphological evolution of coronal mass ejections in the heliosphere.
