title: Uncertainty and metadata projection bottleneck
---

**Background:** Visualizing 2.5-dimensional datasets (e.g., three dimensions where one is sparsely sampled) onto two-dimensional media risks significant information loss or distortion of underlying physical relationships.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing methods to natively encode uncertainty (error bars) and metadata (such as integration times or parameter ranges) into non-standard projection visualizations remains a technical bottleneck for maintaining data integrity during high-dimensional representation.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the challenge of visualizing complex, high-dimensional uncertainty without sacrificing readability or causing excessive visual clutter in non-Cartesian or transformed coordinate systems.

**Evidence:** Future work will aim to include errorbars and a panel-span encoding into LStein.
