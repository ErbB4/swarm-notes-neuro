# Anomalous, pre-yield grain-boundary sliding in copper revealed with in-situ high-resolution strain mapping

**Authors**: Benjamin Poole, David Lunt, Luke Hewitt, Chris Hardie
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.26515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26515)

## Summary

This study investigates room-temperature grain boundary sliding (GBS) in oxygen-free high-conductivity copper using in-situ high-resolution digital image correlation (HRDIC) within a scanning electron microscope. The researchers reveal that GBS initiates before macroscopic yielding and dominates the initial plastic deformation phase, contrary to the typical association of GBS with high-temperature regimes. By synthesizing time-series strain maps with height and orientation data, the authors identify complex pure and mixed-mode sliding behaviors that precede crystallographic slip.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that grain boundary sliding (GBS) occurs extensively prior to macroscopic yield in room-temperature copper.
- Identifies that pre-yield grain boundary sliding produces higher peak strain and in-plane rotation values than crystallographic slip.
- Characterizes GBS modes (in-plane, out-of-plane, and mixed) by integrating HRDIC strain maps with laser scanning confocal microscopy and EBSD data.
