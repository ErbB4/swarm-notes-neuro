# Empirical One-Step Conditional Entropy in Infinite Ergodic Systems: Vanishing Entropy Rate, Sparse-Transition Scaling, and Mittag-Leffler Fluctuations

**Authors**: Ken-ichi Okubo
**Date**: 2026-06-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.05685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05685)

## Summary

This paper investigates the behavior of empirical conditional entropy in weakly chaotic systems characterized by infinite invariant measures. The author proves that standard entropy-rate measures vanish for these intermittent maps, necessitating a focus on finite-time information sums to detect sparse transitions. The work establishes a two-term decay estimate for these sums and connects their fluctuations to Mittag-Leffler distributions, offering a refined perspective on predictability in non-ergodic regimes.

## Key Contributions

- Proves that the per-step empirical conditional entropy converges to zero in one-dimensional intermittent maps with infinite invariant measures, rendering standard entropy-rate normalization ineffective for quantifying instability in these systems.
- Identifies that the finite-time information sum captures sparse transitions between laminar phases, providing a non-vanishing measure of symbolic complexity despite the vanishing entropy rate.
- Demonstrates that fluctuations in the information sum for weak-chaos regimes follow normalized Mittag-Leffler laws, providing a rigorous statistical description of long-range dependence in these dynamics.
