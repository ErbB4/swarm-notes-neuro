# Large values in time series and additive combinatorics

**Authors**: Alex Iosevich, Dr. Vishal Gupta
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.21292](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21292)

## Summary

This paper provides a formal mathematical framework for the observation that large values in time series often exhibit structured, non-random patterns. By applying techniques from additive combinatorics and discrete Fourier analysis, the authors demonstrate that the Fourier ratio serves as a proxy for structural simplicity in the set of extreme values. The study validates this theoretical prediction through experiments on US inflation and Delhi climate data, showing that small generating sets can accurately describe complex sets of extreme values.

## Key Contributions

- Established a theoretical foundation linking extreme values in time series to additive combinatorics using the Fourier ratio.
- Proved that time series with small Fourier ratios have large values that can be generated by a small set of points.
- Demonstrated that large values in inflation and climate datasets are structurally significant and representable by small generating sets (size 4-7).
