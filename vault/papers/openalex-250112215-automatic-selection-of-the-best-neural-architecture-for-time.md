# Automatic selection of the best neural architecture for time series forecasting

**Authors**: Qianying Cao, Shanqing Liu, Alan John Varghese, Jérôme Darbon, Michael S. Triantafyllou, George Em Karniadakis
**Date**: 2026-06-02
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2501.12215](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12215)

## Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of selecting an optimal neural architecture for time series forecasting by proposing an automated framework that constructs and evaluates hybrid models. By employing multi-objective optimization to explore combinations of LSTM, GRU, attention, and state-space model blocks, the framework identifies Pareto-optimal designs tailored to specific application objectives. Additionally, the authors introduce two sampling-based iterative techniques that significantly accelerate the search process, reducing total training costs by nearly eightfold. The results indicate that no single architecture is universally superior, advocating for data-driven, objective-specific architecture search in forecasting tasks.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes an automated framework for designing hybrid time series forecasting models by combining LSTM, GRU, attention, and SSM modules.
- Introduces two sampling-based iterative procedures for Pareto-front exploration that reduce total training cost by nearly eightfold.
- Demonstrates that hybrid architectures outperform monolithic designs when balancing accuracy and complexity requirements across diverse benchmarks.
