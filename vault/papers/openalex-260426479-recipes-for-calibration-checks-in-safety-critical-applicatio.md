# Recipes for Calibration Checks in Safety-Critical Applications

**Authors**: Romeo Valentin
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.26479](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26479)

## Summary

This paper introduces a modular framework for validating the distributional integrity of probabilistic forecasts in safety-critical applications. Unlike traditional methods that yield ambiguous continuous scores, this approach provides actionable accept/reject decisions based on statistical hypothesis tests. The framework is structured into a four-step pipeline with swappable components, allowing it to adapt to diverse operational requirements such as cautious risk-aversion and tolerance for minor distributional deviations. Its utility is demonstrated through practical applications in weather forecasting and robot pose estimation.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a modular calibration checking framework for safety-critical probabilistic forecasting systems.
- Provides a decision-based validation process (accept/reject) that removes the need for expert interpretation of continuous calibration scores.
- Enables risk-aware testing by incorporating mechanisms to tolerate pessimistic forecasts and small, operationally acceptable distribution deviations.
