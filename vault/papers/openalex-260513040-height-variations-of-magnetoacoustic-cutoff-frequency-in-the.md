# Height Variations of Magnetoacoustic Cutoff Frequency in the Solar Atmosphere

**Authors**: Pradeep Kayshap, Gayathri Hegde, Z. E. Musielak, K. Murawski, T. Felipe
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.13040](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13040)

## Summary

This study investigates the vertical variation of the magnetoacoustic cutoff frequency in the solar atmosphere using Doppler velocity time series from IRIS near-ultraviolet spectral lines. By applying cross-wavelet analysis to multiple height layers between the photosphere and the chromosphere, the authors reveal that the cutoff frequency significantly increases with altitude. These results establish a critical observational baseline for constraining and refining theoretical wave dynamics models within the solar atmosphere.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified the magnetoacoustic cutoff frequency at six distinct atmospheric heights using IRIS near ultraviolet spectral lines.
- Demonstrated an increasing trend in cutoff frequency from 3.0 mHz at 0.38 Mm to 8.5 mHz at 1.2 Mm.
- Provided an observational benchmark for validating theoretical models of solar wave propagation in the photosphere-chromosphere transition region.
