# Bandwidth selection with a frequency-domain version of the AIC

**Authors**: Erhard Reschenhofer
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.02295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02295)

## Summary

This paper addresses the lack of standardized, objective methods for automatic bandwidth selection in nonparametric spectral density estimation, which often relies on subjective visual inspection. The author proposes a frequency-domain adaptation of the AIC, providing a computationally simple and reliable alternative for determining smoothing parameters. Through experiments on both synthetic and real-world time series, the study demonstrates that this automated approach yields results competitive with traditional parametric AR model-based estimation.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a frequency-domain version of the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) for automatic bandwidth selection in nonparametric spectral density estimation.
- Demonstrates that the proposed method achieves estimation performance comparable to standard parametric AR-based approaches.
- Provides empirical validation using both synthetic and real-world time series with varying spectral complexity to support objective parameter selection over visual methods.
