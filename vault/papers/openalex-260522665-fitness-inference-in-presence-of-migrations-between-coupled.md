# Fitness Inference in Presence of Migrations between Coupled Evolving Populations

**Authors**: Yu-Han Huang, Bastien Dumont, Hong-Li Zeng, John Barton, Erik Aurell
**Date**: 2026-05-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.22665](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22665)

## Summary

This paper extends the statistical mechanics framework of Quasi-Linkage Equilibrium (QLE) to account for migration between coupled evolutionary populations. By modeling the interplay between selection, recombination, mutation, and migration, the authors identify a regime where QLE persists despite inter-population connectivity. They provide a set of analytical relations that enable precise inference of fitness landscapes, including both additive and epistatic components, from time-series genomic data.

## Key Contributions

- Extends Quasi-Linkage Equilibrium (QLE) theory to coupled populations interacting via symmetric and asymmetric migration.
- Derives analytical inference relations for estimating additive fitness and epistatic interactions in coupled evolutionary systems.
- Validates the persistence of the QLE phase in interconnected populations using whole-genome time-series data.
