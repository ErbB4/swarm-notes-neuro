# Price as Focal Point: Prediction Markets, Conditional Reflexivity, and the Politics of Common Knowledge

**Authors**: Maksym Nechepurenko
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.24147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24147)

## Summary

This paper challenges the view of prediction markets as solely forecasting devices, arguing they often serve as powerful coordination mechanisms that shape political behavior. By analyzing transaction-level data from the 2024 U.S. presidential election, the author introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) to evaluate the conditions under which price signals gain behavioral traction. The results demonstrate that highly visible markets can significantly influence elite coordination even when their predictive accuracy is low, suggesting that social authority and epistemic robustness are distinct, often decoupled, features of these platforms.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) to quantify the behavioral influence of prediction market signals.
- Demonstrates a systematic decoupling of social authority from epistemic robustness in 2024 U.S. election prediction markets.
- Develops a framework to distinguish between market moves that function as forecasting tools versus coordination mechanisms.

## Archivist Review

I approved the Signal Credibility Index as it provides a distinct, quantitative framework for evaluating the behavioral influence of market signals, which is highly relevant to the study of reflexivity in time-series forecasting. The open question regarding causal identification in reflexive markets was approved for its foundational importance in distinguishing between forecasting and coordination effects. I rejected the SCI weighting optimization question as it is a routine empirical task that does not constitute a fundamental, unresolved bottleneck.

### Approved Concepts
- Signal Credibility Index: It provides a novel, microstructure-grounded metric for evaluating the real-world behavioral impact of market signals beyond simple price accuracy, specifically in reflexive market systems.

### Approved Open Questions
- Causal Identification in Reflexivity: Without robust causal identification, it remains impossible to empirically verify the extent to which prediction markets are driving outcomes as opposed to merely reflecting or misinterpreting reality.
