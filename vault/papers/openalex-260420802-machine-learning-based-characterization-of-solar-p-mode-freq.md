# Machine Learning-Based Characterization of Solar p-Mode Frequency Shifts during Solar Cycle 25

**Authors**: Rekha Jain, Akash Kumar, Sushanta C. Tripathy
**Date**: 2026-04-22
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.20802](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20802)

## Summary

This paper explores the temporal relationship between solar p-mode frequency shifts and established solar activity proxies, such as sunspot numbers and 10.7 cm radio flux. The authors employ time-series and machine learning methods to characterize these shifts for the remainder of solar cycle 25. By modeling these shifts, the work aims to enhance the understanding of energy transport from the solar interior to the heliosphere and improve long-term space weather forecasting capabilities.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates the application of standard time-series analysis and machine learning to characterize p-mode solar frequency shifts.
- Identifies p-mode frequency shifts as a potential indicator for solar cycle phases to improve space weather forecasting.
- Establishes a quantitative methodology for bridging solar interior oscillation data with heliospheric activity proxies.
