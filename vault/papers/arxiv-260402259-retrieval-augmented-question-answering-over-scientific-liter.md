# Retrieval-Augmented Question Answering over Scientific Literature for the Electron-Ion Collider

**Authors**: Tina. J. Jat, T. Ghosh, Karthik Suresh
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.02259](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02259)

## Summary

This paper presents a local, privacy-preserving Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system designed to answer domain-specific questions about the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). By utilizing an in-house database of arXiv articles and an open-source LLaMA model, the authors provide a cost-effective and secure alternative to cloud-hosted proprietary models. This approach facilitates the handling of sensitive, pre-publication scientific research while remaining resource-constrained for practical deployment in nuclear physics research environments.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a locally-deployed, resource-constrained Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system tailored for the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) experiment.
- Demonstrated a cost-effective alternative to cloud-hosted proprietary RAG solutions for experimental nuclear physics data.
- Ensured data privacy for sensitive pre-publication scientific information by utilizing an in-house database and open-source LLaMA model.
