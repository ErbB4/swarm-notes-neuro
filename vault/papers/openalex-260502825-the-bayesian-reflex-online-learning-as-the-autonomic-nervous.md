# The Bayesian Reflex: Online Learning as the Autonomic Nervous System of Modern and Future AI

**Authors**: Durba Bhattacharya, Sucharita Roy, Sourabh Bhattacharya
**Date**: 2026-05-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.02825](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02825)

## Summary

This paper presents the 'Bayesian Reflex', a conceptual and computational framework that treats online learning as an autonomic nervous system for AI. By integrating belief maintenance, sequential Bayesian updating, and uncertainty-driven decision-making, the framework enables robust adaptation in dynamic environments. The authors substantiate this approach with two key computational principles—the look-up table principle and ellipsoidal decomposition—and illustrate their versatility across diverse domains including time series, climate modeling, and theoretical mathematics.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the 'Bayesian Reflex' as a unifying architectural framework for online learning in AI, modeling adaptive behavior after the autonomic nervous system.
- Establishes two core computational principles: the look-up table principle for function space inference and the ellipsoidal decomposition framework for nearly exact i.i.d. sampling.
- Demonstrates broad applicability of the framework ranging from deep Recursive Gaussian Processes and restless bandits to novel mathematical discoveries in prime number distribution.

## Archivist Review

The 'Bayesian Reflex' is approved as a high-level conceptual framework for online learning, while the two open questions address critical bottlenecks in scaling Bayesian inference methods (ellipsoidal decomposition and online RGP inference). The 'Look-up table principle' was rejected as it is a broader computational concept better discussed in existing literature rather than a contribution specific to this framework's novelty.

### Approved Concepts
- Bayesian Reflex: Acts as the central unifying framework proposed by the paper to bridge online learning with autonomic nervous system principles.

### Approved Open Questions
- Optimal Ellipsoidal Decomposition Parameters: The efficiency and convergence properties of the perfect sampling algorithm depend critically on the ellipsoidal geometry. Establishing optimal parameters is essential for making this approach scalable and broadly applicable.
- Online Inference for RGPs: Online inference for deep Bayesian models is critical for deploying adaptive AI in dynamic, high-stakes environments where batch retraining is infeasible.
