# Guardrail Selection in Line Charts to Contextualize Persuasive Visualizations

**Authors**: Khandaker Abrar Nadib, Marina Kogan, Alexander Lex, Maxim Lisnic
**Date**: 2026-06-17
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.19017](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19017)

## Summary

This paper investigates the use of contextual guardrails—specifically embedded comparison lines—to mitigate the influence of misleading or cherry-picked persuasive visualizations in time-series data. The authors propose several practical guardrail sampling strategies and evaluate their effectiveness through a user study involving COVID-19 and stock market scenarios. The results show that providing such context significantly enhances viewer accuracy in performance judgments and improves overall trust and perceived chart completeness compared to unguided visualizations.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced a set of guardrail sampling strategies for contextualizing time-series visualizations.
- Demonstrated that embedded comparison lines improve user trust, performance accuracy, and perceived completeness in persuasive charts.
- Conducted a preregistered mixed-design user study evaluating the impact of guardrails on COVID-19 and stock market data visualizations.
