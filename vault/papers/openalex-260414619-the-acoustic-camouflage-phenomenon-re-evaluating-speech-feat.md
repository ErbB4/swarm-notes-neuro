# The Acoustic Camouflage Phenomenon: Re-evaluating Speech Features for Financial Risk Prediction

**Authors**: Dhruvin Dungrani, Disha Dungrani
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.14619](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14619)

## Summary

This study examines the effectiveness of acoustic feature extraction for predicting financial tail-risk events in corporate earnings calls. By comparing a two-stream late-fusion architecture against an NLP-only baseline, the researchers discovered that acoustic features significantly reduced predictive recall. They define this performance degradation as Acoustic Camouflage, attributing it to vocal regulation in trained speakers that introduces noise into multimodal models. These findings challenge the assumption that standard paralinguistic markers for deception or cognitive load generalize to high-stakes, media-trained environments.

## Key Contributions

- Identified the Acoustic Camouflage phenomenon as a failure mode for paralinguistic features in professional environments.
- Demonstrated that integrating acoustic features with NLP models can degrade recall in financial tail-risk prediction from 66.25% to 47.08%.
- Established a boundary condition for the utility of speech processing in corporate earnings call analysis.
