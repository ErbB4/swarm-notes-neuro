# HLS-GPT: A Generative Pretrained Transformer (GPT) for Continental-Scale NASA Harmonized Landsat and Sentinel-2 (HLS) Reflectance Reconstruction Across All Bands on Arbitrary Dates

**Authors**: Junjie Li, Hankui K. Zhang, David P. Roy
**Date**: 2026-06-16
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.18115](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18115)

## Summary

HLS-GPT is a large-scale generative pretrained transformer designed to reconstruct Harmonized Landsat and Sentinel-2 (HLS) surface reflectance for any date and pixel location. Utilizing a hierarchical architecture, the model processes 12-month single-pixel time series to capture seasonal and geographic variability across the conterminous United States. By training on a large-scale dataset with a masking strategy, the model achieves highly accurate spectral reconstruction even under conditions of sparse and irregular data availability.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces HLS-GPT, a transformer-based generative model for single-pixel continental-scale surface reflectance reconstruction across all HLS bands.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on HLS time series reconstruction, outperforming conventional methods and the NASA-IBM Prithvi model.
- Demonstrates robust performance under sparse and irregular observation regimes, maintaining consistent reconstruction accuracy across varying masking levels.
