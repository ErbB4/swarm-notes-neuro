# Negative redispatch power for green hydrogen production: Game changer or lame duck? A German perspective

**Authors**: Jonathan Brandt, Astrid Bensmann, Richard Hanke‐Rauschenbach
**Date**: 2026-04-15
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2508.06500](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.06500)

## Summary

This paper investigates the economic potential of integrating water electrolysers into German regional redispatch markets to utilize curtailed renewable energy for green hydrogen production. By modeling various project configurations and electricity sourcing scenarios against historical redispatch time series, the authors determine the conditions under which redispatch power provides a viable cost-reduction incentive. The findings suggest that the competitiveness of green hydrogen is highly sensitive to redispatch price levels, with low prices fostering system-beneficial siting while high prices discourage participation.

## Key Contributions

- Evaluates the economic viability of green hydrogen production using negative redispatch power in the German transmission network.
- Develops a project-level modeling framework to assess the impact of different power purchase options, market prices, and regulatory conditions on hydrogen production costs.
- Demonstrates that while low redispatch price levels can significantly reduce production costs and incentivize system-beneficial electrolyser siting, high price levels effectively negate these economic advantages.
