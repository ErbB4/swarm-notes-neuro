# A New Member of the Fast and Furious Family: A Relativistic and Time-Variable UV Outflow in a Luminous Quasar

**Authors**: Lucas M. Seaton, Patrick B. Hall, Liliana Flores, Paola Rodríguez Hidalgo, Marianna Veltri, Zi Zhu, Javier Serna, Javier Serna, W. Niel Brandt, Scott Anderson, Roberto J. Trump, Erik Weiss
**Date**: 2026-06-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.06226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06226)

## Summary

This paper presents the discovery of an exceptionally fast, relativistic ultraviolet outflow in the quasar SDSS J231854.31+243954.2, with velocities reaching approximately 0.3c. Through multi-epoch spectroscopy and long-term photometry, the authors observe monotonic strengthening of C IV absorption, challenging existing models of high-velocity ion persistence. The calculated kinetic luminosity ratio indicates that this outflow likely exerts significant feedback on the quasar's host galaxy.

## Key Contributions

- Identified and characterized a relativistic ultraviolet outflow in the quasar SDSS J231854.31+243954.2 reaching velocities of at least -90,000 km/s (0.3c).
- Demonstrated that the outflow's kinetic luminosity ratio (L_kin/L_bol >= 0.75%) suggests significant potential feedback on the host galaxy.
- Documented monotonic strengthening of the C IV absorption feature over a 2.2-year rest-frame period, providing new constraints for quasar outflow acceleration models.
