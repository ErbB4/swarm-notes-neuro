# Universal Features in Atmospheric Particulate Matter Dynamics

**Authors**: Suchismita Banerjee, Koyena Ghosh, Urna Basu, Banasri Basu
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.25386](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25386)

## Summary

This paper analyzes six years of daily PM2.5 concentration data across 54 Indian cities to uncover universal statistical properties in atmospheric particulate matter dynamics. By decomposing the time series to isolate residual fluctuations, the authors observe that these signals exhibit consistent probability density functions and spectral decay patterns, despite significant environmental heterogeneity. To account for these findings, the study introduces a minimal stochastic model that successfully captures the observed stationary distributions, temporal autocorrelations, and 1/f spectral scaling.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies universal statistical and dynamical patterns in PM2.5 fluctuations across 54 heterogeneous Indian cities over a six-year period.
- Demonstrates that rescaled residual PM2.5 fluctuations follow an exponentially modified Gaussian distribution regardless of local urban or climatic conditions.
- Develops a minimal stochastic model that replicates observed power-law spectral density decay and temporal correlation characteristics.
