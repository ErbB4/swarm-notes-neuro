# Nonlinear and Heavy-Tailed Predictability in Transition-Energy Financial Markets

**Authors**: Kpante Emmanuel Gnandi, Fredy Pokou, Jules Sadefo Kamdem
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.26890](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26890)

## Summary

This paper addresses the limitations of conventional Gaussian-linear models in forecasting transition-energy financial markets, which are characterized by abrupt repricing and heavy-tailed volatility. The authors introduce a hybrid model that combines Student-t VAR with nonlinear recurrent neural networks to specifically filter out linear dynamics before learning residual nonlinear patterns. Extensive empirical testing on six sector-specific exchange-traded funds confirms that this approach improves forecast accuracy, especially during significant economic shocks. The results highlight the necessity of accounting for both heavy-tailed distributions and nonlinear dependencies when modeling modern energy transition assets.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a hybrid forecasting framework that integrates Student-t Vector Autoregressions (VAR) with nonlinear recurrent residual learning to capture heavy-tailed dependencies.
- Demonstrated that transition-related financial returns contain residual nonlinear predictability that standard Gaussian-linear models fail to capture.
- Validated that the hybrid model significantly outperforms traditional VAR, standalone machine learning, and existing hybrid baselines, particularly during periods of high macro-financial stress.
