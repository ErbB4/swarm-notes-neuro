# Decoding Latent Spaces: Assessing the Interpretability of Time Series Foundation Models for Visual Analytics

**Authors**: Inmaculada Santamaria-Valenzuela, Víctor Rodríguez-Fernández, Javier Huertas‐Tato, Jong Hyuk Park, David Camacho
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2504.20099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.20099)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the interpretability of latent spaces in the MOMENT time series foundation model family across diverse tasks like forecasting, imputation, and anomaly detection. The study investigates whether fine-tuning improves the structural clarity of latent embeddings for visual analysis, finding that while fine-tuning enhances predictive accuracy, it fails to significantly improve the visual interpretability of these spaces. The authors conclude that further methodological refinements, such as specialized loss functions or projection techniques, are necessary to bridge the gap between model performance and human-interpretable latent representations for visual analytics.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic evaluation of the latent space interpretability of the MOMENT time series foundation model family.
- Empirical analysis showing that while fine-tuning reduces task-specific loss, it does not consistently improve the interpretability of projected latent embeddings for visual analytics.
- Identification of performance gains in computational execution time, establishing foundation models as efficient candidates for interactive visual analytics despite current latent space limitations.
