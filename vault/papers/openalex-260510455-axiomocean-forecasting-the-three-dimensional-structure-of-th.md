# AxiomOcean: Forecasting the Three-Dimensional Structure of the Upper Ocean

**Authors**: Sensen Wu, Yifan Chen, Guantao Pu, Xiaoyao Sun, Yijun Chen, Jin Qi, Ming Kong, Keyi Yang, Lichen Xu, Wenguan Wang, Xiaofeng Li, Zhenhong Du
**Date**: 2026-05-11
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.10455](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10455)

## Summary

AxiomOcean is a global AI forecasting model designed to address the limitations of existing AI approaches in preserving three-dimensional subsurface ocean structures. By utilizing a 3D encoder-backbone-decoder architecture, the model incorporates vertical hierarchy and cross-layer dependencies to predict temperature, salinity, and currents down to 643 meters. It demonstrates superior accuracy and physical realism compared to state-of-the-art AI benchmarks across key oceanic regions like the equatorial Pacific and the Southern Ocean.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces AxiomOcean, a 3D AI-based forecasting model that explicitly models vertical hierarchies and cross-layer dependencies in the ocean column.
- Reduces day-1 RMSE by 20–35% compared to baseline AI models in 10-day global ocean forecasts at 1/12° resolution.
- Maintains physical fidelity (e.g., eddy kinetic energy, temperature/salinity variance) through improved architectural design, avoiding over-smoothing common in standard AI forecasting methods.
