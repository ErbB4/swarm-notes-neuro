# Scenario Generation for Time Series and Curves: A Comparison of Nonparametric and Semiparametric Bootstrap

**Authors**: Nicola Baldoni, Michele Sparviero, Lorenzo Viola
**Date**: 2026-06-10
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.11859](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11859)

## Summary

This paper evaluates and contrasts nonparametric and semiparametric bootstrap techniques for generating stochastic asset-class trajectories. While traditional nonparametric stationary bootstrap methods successfully maintain historical return distributions, they often fail to generate paths that are economically plausible, particularly in fixed-income settings. By combining parametric models—such as autoregressive or mean-reverting structures—with the resampling of historical residuals, the authors show that semiparametric approaches can effectively bridge the gap between distributional accuracy and path-wise realism.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic comparison of nonparametric versus semiparametric bootstrap methods for stochastic trajectory generation in quantitative finance.
- Demonstration that semiparametric bootstrap methods preserve both historical return distributions and path-wise economic realism for interest rates and yield curves.
- Validation that integrating parametric term-structure models with residual resampling produces more coherent yield-curve simulations compared to traditional stationary bootstrap approaches.
