# System Level Analysis and Management of Orbital Debris Using Empirical Dynamic Modeling

**Authors**: Asaad S. Abdul-Hamid, Hao Chen
**Date**: 2026-05-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.21892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21892)

## Summary

This paper addresses the growing orbital debris crisis by applying Empirical Dynamic Modeling (EDM) to simulate the system-level impacts of space policy. By identifying causal relationships between debris counts, total objects, and launch frequencies, the authors reconstruct a shadow attractor to model the system's underlying dynamics. This approach allows for long-term forecasting and policy evaluation in data-limited scenarios where traditional physics-based models may struggle to capture emergent complexity. The proposed method provides a novel, complexity-science-based tool for orbital management and infrastructure sustainability.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a data-driven framework for orbital debris management using Empirical Dynamic Modeling (EDM) to treat debris evolution as a dynamic system.
- Demonstrates reconstruction of a shadow attractor from limited observable variables to identify causal links between debris, objects, and launch rates.
- Enables policy impact simulation by predicting system-level future states of orbital debris based on dynamic manifold trajectories.
