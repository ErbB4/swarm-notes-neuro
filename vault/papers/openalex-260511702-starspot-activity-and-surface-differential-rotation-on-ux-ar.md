# Starspot activity and surface differential rotation on UX Arietis

**Authors**: Yue Xiang, Shenghong Gu, A. Collier Cameron, J. R. Barnes, Dongtao Cao
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.11702](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11702)

## Summary

This study presents new Doppler imaging of the primary K0 subgiant in the UX Arietis binary system based on high-resolution spectra from 2017 and 2024. The authors refine the Doppler reconstruction process by using the Ca I 6439 Å line as a reference to improve the reliability of least-squares deconvolution profiles. The resulting surface maps reveal significant mid-to-high latitude starspot activity and indicate a weak anti-solar differential rotation for the star. Additionally, the study finds a spatial association between a primary starspot group in late 2017 and a subsequent major stellar flare.

## Key Contributions

- Reconstructed Doppler images of the K0 subgiant component of UX Arietis using both Ca I 6439 Å line profiles and least-squares deconvolution (LSD).
- Demonstrated that surface starspot structures at mid-to-high latitudes shifted by approximately 0.5 in rotational phase between 2017 and 2024.
- Identified a weak anti-solar differential rotation for the primary component of UX Arietis via cross-correlation of Doppler maps.
