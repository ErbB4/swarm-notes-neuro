# Conflict Forecasting via Conformal Prediction for Markov Processes

**Authors**: Aditya Basarkar, Emmett B. Kendall, David Randahl, Jonathan P. Williams, Gudmund H. Hermansen
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.25139](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25139)

## Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of forecasting conflict dynamics by employing conformal prediction to generate valid uncertainty sets for future state-sequences in Markov processes. Unlike traditional point-prediction methods, which may carry extreme risks in geopolitical contexts, this approach provides statistically valid coverage even under potential model misspecification. The authors evaluate the methodology against likelihood-based strategies and offer a critical discussion on the theoretical hurdles of applying conformal prediction to non-exchangeable temporal data.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a conformal prediction framework for generating valid uncertainty sets for future conflict state-sequences in Markovian processes.
- Provides a robust alternative to likelihood-based prediction strategies, addressing risks of model misspecification in high-stakes conflict forecasting.
- Critically analyzes the applicability of standard conformal prediction methods to non-exchangeable, temporally-dependent Markovian data.
