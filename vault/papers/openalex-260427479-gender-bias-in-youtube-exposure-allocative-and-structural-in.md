# Gender Bias in YouTube Exposure: Allocative and Structural Inequalities in Political Information Environments

**Authors**: Jipeng Tan, Weifeng Zhang, Ye Wu, Jialin Guo, Yŏng Min
**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.27479](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27479)

## Summary

This paper investigates gender-based algorithmic bias in YouTube's political recommendation environment using a controlled social-bot field experiment. The authors identify both allocative biases—differences in content, ideology, and entities—and structural biases characterized by community-level clustering. By modeling these dynamics, the study illustrates how recommendation algorithms reinforce societal inequalities through personalized information exposure pathways.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies statistically significant gender-based allocative bias in YouTube recommendations regarding issue distribution, ideology, and political entities via controlled bot experiments.
- Demonstrates structural bias on YouTube through distinct clustering patterns that differentially segregate male-coded and female-coded profiles.
- Provides a collaborative-filtering model that successfully reproduces observed gender-based inequalities in political information exposure.
