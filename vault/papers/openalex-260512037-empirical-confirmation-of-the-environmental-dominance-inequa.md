# Empirical Confirmation of the Environmental-Dominance Inequality A direct decomposition of Var(ln \r{ho}eff ) across four levels of aggregation

**Authors**: Kristian Sestak
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.12037](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12037)

## Summary

This paper provides an empirical verification of the environmental-dominance inequality, which asserts that the variance of effective density significantly outweighs that of intrinsic capability. By analyzing three public datasets at four aggregation levels—ranging from global individual distributions to U.S. census tracts—the author quantifies the dominance ratio and investigates its temporal stability. The results show that while the global aggregate remains stable, internal composition shifts between 1990 and 2022 confirm patterns of international convergence and domestic divergence. The study also confirms the robustness of this inequality across diverse socioeconomic metrics such as income and mortality rates.

## Key Contributions

- Provides empirical validation for the environmental-dominance inequality, demonstrating that effective density variance consistently dwarfs intrinsic capability variance (Var(ln ρ_eff) ≫ Var(ln k)).
- Quantifies the dominance ratio across four levels of aggregation, revealing a shift from between-country to within-country dispersion between 1990 and 2022.
- Demonstrates the robustness of the environmental-dominance effect across distinct socioeconomic indicators, including income, infant mortality, and incarceration.
