# Human-AI Co-Mentorship in Project-Based Learning: A Case Study in Financial Forecasting

**Authors**: Freyaa Chawla, Ahan Chawla, Rishi Singh, Joe Germino, Grigorii Khvatskii
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.05144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05144)

## Summary

This paper presents a pedagogical case study on using AI tools to facilitate research projects for students with limited prior technical experience. By emphasizing a workflow-first approach, the project enabled high-school participants to focus on high-level problem formulation and financial analysis while offloading technical implementation to AI assistants. The study demonstrates that such co-mentorship models effectively bridge skill gaps and accelerate learning in complex domains like financial forecasting.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a pedagogical framework where students leverage AI tools to execute technical workflows, allowing focus on high-level problem formulation rather than prerequisite mastery.
- Reports on a case study where students with limited technical backgrounds successfully developed functional financial models for ETF price prediction.
- Introduces a 'workflow-first' pedagogical model that integrates daily human-to-human stand-ups for conceptual debugging and AI-assisted iterative coding.
