# Quo vadis, stochastic thermodynamics?

**Authors**: Jan Korbel, Artemy Kolchinsky, Sarah A. M. Loos, Gonzalo Manzano, Rosalba Garcia-Millan, Olga Movilla Miangolarra, Édgar Roldán
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.26601](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26601)

## Summary

This perspective reviews the evolution of stochastic thermodynamics, a framework that models system dynamics as stochastic time series to quantify thermodynamic observables like entropy production along individual trajectories. The authors detail advancements in applying these principles to complex systems, including those with memory, interacting particles, and geometric structures defined via optimal transport. Furthermore, the paper critically examines the challenges of scaling these concepts to macroscopic complex systems and explores their utility in non-physical fields such as information processing and socio-biological dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive review of recent extensions of stochastic thermodynamics to systems with memory, hidden degrees of freedom, and active matter.
- Synthesizes the theoretical challenges in bridging statistical irreversibility and thermodynamic dissipation in complex macroscopic systems.
- Discusses emerging cross-disciplinary applications of stochastic thermodynamics in biology, computation, and social dynamics.
