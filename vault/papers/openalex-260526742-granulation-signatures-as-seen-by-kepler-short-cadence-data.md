# Granulation signatures as seen by Kepler short-cadence data. II. A hierarchical route to inferring stellar radii from granulation

**Authors**: Jens R. Larsen, Mia S. Lundkvist, Guy R. Davies, Martin B. Nielsen, 
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.26742](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26742)

## Summary

This paper presents a Bayesian hierarchical framework to infer stellar radii from near-surface convection patterns observed in high-cadence photometric time series. By modeling the relationship between stellar radius and features like total granulation amplitude and characteristic frequency, the approach effectively accounts for parameter uncertainty and intrinsic dispersion. The method achieves approximately 10% precision, demonstrating that granulation acts as a predictive stellar diagnostic comparable to existing techniques for mission data such as Kepler and TESS.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a Bayesian hierarchical model for inferring stellar radii directly from photometric granulation signatures.
- Validates the approach on a sample of 367 stars, achieving ~10% precision in radius estimation.
- Demonstrates that stellar granulation serves as a viable structural diagnostic for Kepler, TESS, and PLATO mission data.
