# Towards personalised intervention: A causal-dynamical framework to determine psychological treatment trajectories

**Authors**: Lourens Waldorp, Titus Mürtz, Anita Jansen, Jonas Haslbeck
**Date**: 2026-06-08
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2606.09283](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09283)

## Summary

This paper proposes a causal-dynamical framework for personalized mental health treatment to address the limitations of diagnostic-based clinical decision-making. By leveraging longitudinal data, the approach constructs individual causal graphs to simulate treatment trajectories and compare various intervention strategies. This framework allows clinicians to identify the most effective intervention focus for an individual patient, moving beyond population-average treatment guidelines.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a causal-dynamical framework for personalizing mental health treatment by shifting from diagnostic-based to simulation-based decision-making.
- Introduces an end-to-end process: learning causal graphs from longitudinal patient data, simulating patient-specific intervention trajectories, and evaluating treatment efficacy.
- Demonstrates a method to mitigate clinical bias and account for individual heterogeneity in responsiveness by comparing predicted long-term outcomes of different intervention strategies.
