## Summary

This paper details the discovery and dynamical characterization of the TOI-5624 multi-planet system using space-based photometry and ground-based radial velocity observations. By integrating N-body equations of motion with transit-timing variation (TTV) analysis and radial velocity modeling, the authors confirm the presence of four transiting sub-Neptunes and an additional non-transiting outer companion. The study achieves exceptionally high precision in planetary radii measurements, comparable only to the TRAPPIST-1 system.

## Key Contributions

- Discovery and characterization of a multi-planet system TOI-5624 containing four transiting sub-Neptunes and one non-transiting outer companion.
- Demonstration of precise planet mass and radius estimation using combined TESS/CHEOPS photometry and HARPS-N/SOPHIE radial velocity data within an MCMC framework.
- Utilization of transit-timing variations (TTVs) and N-body simulations to infer the existence and properties of a non-transiting planet.
