# Axion-Like Particle Dark Matter Intensity Mapping: A New Probe via Cross-Correlation with Galaxy Surveys

**Authors**: Wen-Qing Guo
**Date**: 2026-05-07
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.05906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05906)

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel cosmological framework to detect μeV-scale Axion-like particle (ALP) dark matter using cross-correlation between radio intensity mapping and galaxy distribution data. By accounting for stimulated decay effects from both the Cosmic Microwave Background and the extragalactic radio background, the research establishes a robust theoretical model for dark matter detection. Forecasts indicate that the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) Phase 2 could serve as a powerful instrument for this approach, offering a distinct and complementary probe to current dark matter search strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a novel method to probe μeV-scale Axion-like particle (ALP) dark matter through the cross-correlation of radio intensity mapping and galaxy surveys.
- Develops a comprehensive theoretical model incorporating ALP stimulated decay effects driven by both the Cosmic Microwave Background and the extragalactic radio background.
- Demonstrates the sensitivity of SKA Phase 2 for detecting ALP dark matter signals, providing a competitive alternative to existing search methodologies.
