# Hot Rocks Survey V: Secondary Eclipse Photometry of GJ 3473 b with JWST/MIRI

**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.02332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02332)

## Summary

This study presents the first secondary eclipse observations of the rocky exoplanet GJ 3473 b using JWST/MIRI F1500W photometry. The measured eclipse depth of 186±45 ppm is consistent with both airless surface models and thin-atmosphere scenarios, while allowing for the exclusion of thick CO2 atmospheres. The analysis highlights significant observational challenges, including detector settling effects and potential visit-to-visit variability, underscoring the limitations of single-filter photometry in characterizing the atmospheres of highly irradiated rocky planets.

## Key Contributions

- Reports the first JWST/MIRI F1500W secondary eclipse photometry of the rocky exoplanet GJ 3473 b, measured at 186±45 ppm.
- Establishes a 95% credible upper limit of 1.2–6.5 bar on surface pressure, effectively excluding thick CO2 atmospheres.
- Identifies detector settling behaviors and potential visit-to-visit eclipse depth variability, providing methodological guidance for future MIRI exoplanet observations.

## Limitations

The current data suffer from intrinsic degeneracies that prevent a definitive distinction between a bare-rock surface and a thin atmospheric scenario; tentative variability in eclipse depth requires further confirmation.
