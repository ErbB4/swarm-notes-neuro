# Forecasting synchrotron spectral parameters with QUIJOTE-MFI2 in combination with Planck and WMAP

**Authors**: Ana Sofia Almeida, J. A. Rubiño-Martín, Roke Cepeda-Arroita, R. T. Génova-Santos, Debabrata Adak
**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2511.14572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14572)

## Summary

This paper presents a parametric component separation forecast for the QUIJOTE-MFI2 instrument, assessing its efficacy in constraining polarized synchrotron emission parameters when combined with existing WMAP and Planck data. Using simulated sky maps featuring both power-law and curved spectra, the authors quantify uncertainty reductions for synchrotron spectral index, curvature, and polarization amplitudes. The results indicate that QUIJOTE-MFI2 significantly improves parameter estimation in deep cosmological fields and reduces synchrotron residuals at 100 GHz, highlighting its role in refining Galactic emission models for future CMB surveys.

## Key Contributions

- Evaluates the impact of integrating QUIJOTE-MFI2 data with WMAP and Planck for constraining polarized synchrotron emission spectral parameters.
- Demonstrates significant uncertainty reductions, achieving improvement factors of ~10 for spectral index (β_s), ~5 for curvature (C_s), and ~43 for polarization amplitudes in bright regions.
- Establishes that the combination reduces the median synchrotron residual at 100 GHz by a factor of six, providing critical low-frequency benchmarks for future CMB missions like LiteBIRD and the Simons Observatory.
