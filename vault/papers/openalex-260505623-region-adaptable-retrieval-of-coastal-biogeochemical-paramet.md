# Region-adaptable retrieval of coastal biogeochemical parameters from near-surface hyperspectral remote sensing reflectance using physics-aware meta-learning

**Authors**: Yiqing Guo, Nagur Cherukuru, Eric A. Lehmann, S. L. Kesav Unnithan, Tim Malthus, Gemma Kerrisk, Xiubin Qi, Faisal Islam, Tisham Dhar, Mark Doubell
**Date**: 2026-05-07
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.05623](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05623)

## Summary

This paper introduces a two-stage physics-aware meta-learning framework designed to address the challenge of generalizing biogeochemical (BGC) parameter retrieval across diverse coastal water bodies. The approach leverages bio-optical forward models to generate physically informed synthetic data for pretraining a base model, which is subsequently fine-tuned on local in situ measurements. Experimental results across five distinct Australian coastal sites demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in capturing regional bio-optical variations and outperforming conventional baseline models in both magnitude and temporal dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed a two-stage physics-aware meta-learning framework for retrieving coastal biogeochemical parameters from hyperspectral reflectance.
- Demonstrated that synthetic datasets generated by bio-optical forward models provide sufficient physical prior for training region-agnostic models.
- Achieved superior retrieval accuracy compared to five baseline models across five distinct Australian coastal sites.
