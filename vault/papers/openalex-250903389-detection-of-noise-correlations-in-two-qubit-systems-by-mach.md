# Detection of noise correlations in two qubit systems by machine learning

**Authors**: Dario Fasone, Shreyasi Mukherjee, Dario Penna, Fabio Cirinnà, Mauro Paternostro, Elisabetta Paladino, Luigi Giannelli, G. Falci
**Date**: 2026-04-21
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2509.03389](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.03389)

## Summary

This paper proposes a machine learning-based quantum sensing protocol to detect and classify different types of noise in two-qubit systems. By leveraging the sensitivity of coherent population transfer efficiencies under specific driving conditions, the method distinguishes between six classes of Markovian and non-Markovian noise with high accuracy (>94%). The approach is notable for its efficiency, as it circumvents the need for real-time monitoring or time-series data collection by utilizing a shallow neural network. This framework demonstrates how machine learning can enhance the characterization of quantum hardware non-idealities.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a machine-learning assisted quantum sensing protocol for classifying spatial and temporal correlations of classical noise in two-qubit systems.
- Achieves >94% classification accuracy for six distinct Markovian and non-Markovian noise classes using only final transfer efficiencies from a coherent population transfer protocol.
- Reduces experimental resource requirements by avoiding time-series measurements, relying instead on a shallow neural network trained on inputs from specific driving conditions.
