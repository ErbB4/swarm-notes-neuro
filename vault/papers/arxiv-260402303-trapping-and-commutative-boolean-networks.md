# Trapping and commutative Boolean networks

**Authors**: Maximilien Gadouleau
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.02303](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02303)

## Summary

This paper establishes a formal connection between the trapspace structure of Boolean networks (BNs) and their update dynamics by introducing the trapping graph and trapping closure. The author proves that the trapping closure completely captures the principal trapspace distribution of a BN and shows that commutative networks inherently possess a trapping structure. Furthermore, the work classifies specific subsets of commutative networks, namely bijective (Marseille) and idempotent (Lille) networks, providing comprehensive characterizations for their trapspace collections and underlying graph dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced the trapping graph and trapping closure, demonstrating that these uniquely determine the principal trapspace structure of a Boolean network.
- Proved that all commutative Boolean networks are trapping and fully classified their principal trapspace collections.
- Defined and characterized 'Marseille networks' (bijective commutative) and 'Lille networks' (idempotent commutative) as distinct subclasses within the trapping network framework.

## Limitations

The theoretical focus is limited to the algebraic and combinatorial properties of Boolean networks; the paper does not explore specific physical or biological applications of these new network classes.
