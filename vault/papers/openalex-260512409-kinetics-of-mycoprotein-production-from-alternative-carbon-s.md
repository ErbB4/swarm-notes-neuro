# Kinetics of Mycoprotein Production from Alternative Carbon Substrates

**Authors**: Mason Banks, Nipon Sarmah, Yiying He, Thomas Vinestock, Mark Taylor, Miao Guo
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.12409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12409)

## Summary

This study investigates the biokinetics of *F. venenatum* A3/5 growth on diverse carbon sources using high-throughput screening and modified Gompertz models. Researchers analyzed how substrate composition influences carbon allocation strategies, discovering that while simple sugars promote rapid growth, they often trigger fermentative byproduct formation and inhibit secondary substrate efficiency in dual-substrate environments. The findings highlight the significant impact of metabolic inheritance on growth phenotypes and identify waste-stream functional drinks as a highly effective, sustainable substrate for mycoprotein production.

## Key Contributions

- Applied high-throughput screening and modified multiphase Gompertz modeling to characterize F. venenatum growth kinetics across varied carbon substrates.
- Identified metabolic inheritance and environmental shift effects as key kinetic bottlenecks in dual-substrate systems, where glucose-dominated primary growth impairs secondary substrate efficiency.
- Demonstrated that expired functional drink outperforms synthetic counterparts in biomass titre and growth rate, while minimizing fermentative byproduct formation.
