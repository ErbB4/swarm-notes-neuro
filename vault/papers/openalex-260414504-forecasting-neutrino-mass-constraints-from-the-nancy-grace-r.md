# Forecasting neutrino mass constraints from the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

**Authors**: Francesco Spezzati, Yun Wang, Andrew Hearin
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.14504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14504)

## Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive forecast for the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope's ability to constrain the sum of neutrino masses using galaxy power spectrum analysis. The authors employ both an EFT of LSS-based model within ΛCDM and a model-independent phenomenological framework to evaluate constraints across the redshift range 0.5 < z < 2. Results indicate that Roman is expected to reach competitive precision on key cosmological parameters, with neutrino mass constraints sensitive to the inclusion of external priors like those from Planck.

## Key Contributions

- Provides realistic forecasts for neutrino mass constraints using the Roman Space Telescope's High Latitude Wide Area Spectroscopic Survey data.
- Demonstrates neutrino mass sensitivity of mν < 0.276 eV (95% C.L.) using EFT of LSS within ΛCDM with Planck priors.
- Validates a model-independent phenomenological approach that recovers unbiased cosmological parameters and achieves mν < 0.63 eV (95% C.L.) without assuming ΛCDM background.
