# From 'Here' to 'There': Exploring Proximity Semantics in Multimodal Data Exploration

**Authors**: Dennis Bromley, Diana Wang, Vidya Setlur
**Date**: 2026-05-04
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2605.02261](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02261)

## Summary

This paper explores how users interact with time-series and geospatial data using multimodal inputs, specifically focusing on the intersection of sketching, natural language, and visual annotations. The authors introduce a hybrid architecture that combines geometric sketch matching with visual language models to interpret these interleaved query methods. Through a preliminary study, they identify 'Proximity Semantics' (PS), a phenomenon where the spatial and temporal closeness of multimodal elements serves as a critical mechanism for disambiguating analytical intent. The work establishes PS as a foundational lens for the design of future multimodal data exploration systems.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a multimodal research probe integrating sketching, natural language, and visual annotations for time-series and geospatial data exploration.
- Proposes 'Proximity Semantics' (PS) as a conceptual framework for deictic disambiguation in multimodal human-computer interaction.
- Demonstrates through user study that participants naturally rely on relative placement of inputs to resolve ambiguity in analytical queries.
