# Statistical Study of Balmer Continuum Enhancement in Solar Flares

**Authors**: Pranjali Sharma, Lucia Kleint, Jonas Zbinden
**Date**: 2026-04-13
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2604.11276](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11276)

## Summary

This study provides a statistical analysis of near-ultraviolet (NUV) continuum enhancements in solar flares using 234 IRIS observations. The authors developed a robust dual-pipeline detection method to identify and validate enhancement events based on their spatial and temporal correspondence with FUV emission. Results indicate that these enhancements occur primarily at flare ribbon edges and show a clear correlation between flare class and enhancement magnitude, offering critical constraints for solar flare energy transport models.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified NUV continuum enhancement statistics using 234 IRIS solar flare observations.
- Developed a dual-pipeline detection framework utilizing spatio-temporal correlation between NUV and FUV to filter false positives.
- Demonstrated that NUV enhancement magnitude correlates with flare class, predominantly localizing at flare ribbon edges.
