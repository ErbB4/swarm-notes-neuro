# How Well Do Molecular Dynamics Force Fields Model Peptides? A Systematic Benchmark Across Diverse Folding Behaviors

**Authors**: Singh, B., Martinez-Noa, Y., perez, a.
**Date**: 2026-04-01
**Paper ID**: [biorxiv:10.1101/2025.07.31.667969](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.07.31.667969)

## Summary

This paper presents a systematic benchmarking framework for twelve fixed-charge force fields applied to peptides with diverse folding behaviors, ranging from stable miniproteins to disordered sequences. By comparing folded and extended simulations, the authors characterize force field biases and demonstrate that no single model consistently captures the full spectrum of conformational plasticity. The results highlight a critical limitation in current models regarding the balance of secondary structure and intrinsic disorder, providing a necessary reference for future force field development.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic evaluation of twelve fixed-charge force fields across diverse peptide conformational regimes.
- Performance characterization of force fields using dual-state (folded and extended) simulations, covering structured miniproteins and disordered sequences.
- Identification of persistent structural biases and trade-offs in current force fields between modeling disorder and stable secondary structures.
